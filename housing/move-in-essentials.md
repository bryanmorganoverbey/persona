# Move-In Essentials

Items the property must provide or that need to be acquired on day one. Broken down by what's expected from the property vs. what to bring or buy.

## property must provide

### kitchen
- Full-size refrigerator with freezer and ice cube maker
- Full-size stove (at least two burners)
- Oven
- Full-size sink
- Blender
- Coffee pot
- Pots (at least 2 sizes)
- Pans (at least 1 skillet and 1 saucepan)
- Full cutlery set: forks, knives, spoons (service for at least 4)
- Cooking utensils: ladles, spatula, serving spoons, tongs
- Plates, bowls, cups, glasses (service for at least 4)
- Cutting board
- Can opener
- Dish soap and sponge

### bathroom
- Towels: minimum 3 per person (6 total)
- Toilet paper (initial supply)
- Soap or body wash

### bedroom
- Bed frame and mattress (clean, no bed bugs)
- Pillows (2 per person)
- Sheets and pillowcases
- Blanket or comforter

### laundry
- Washing machine
- Dryer (US) or clothesline/drying rack (Mexico)
- Laundry detergent (initial supply)

### climate
- Functioning A/C unit

## bring or buy on day one

### personal
- Toiletries (toothbrush, toothpaste, shampoo, etc.)
- Medications
- Personal electronics and chargers
- Work laptop and peripherals

### workspace
- Two desks (if property is unfurnished)
- Desk chairs (if property is unfurnished)
- Monitor or external display
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset for video calls

### household
- Trash bags
- Paper towels
- Basic cleaning supplies (all-purpose cleaner, broom, mop)
- Hangers for closet

### groceries (day one basics)
- Drinking water (especially in Mexico if no filtration)
- Coffee or tea
- Milk or creamer
- Bread
- Eggs
- Fruit
- Cooking oil
- Salt and pepper

### connectivity
- Test internet speed immediately upon arrival
- Have a mobile hotspot as backup for the first few days
