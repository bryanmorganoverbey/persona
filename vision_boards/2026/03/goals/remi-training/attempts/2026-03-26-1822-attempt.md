## 2. Training Protocol for Consistent Calm Behavior

### Phase 1: Consolidation at Familiar Locations (Weeks 1-2)

**Objective:** Achieve reliable, consistent calm behavior at previously visited cafes where Remi has already been trained.

**Session Structure:**
1. **Arrival Protocol**
   - Allow brief settling period (2-3 minutes)
   - Give Remi water, but no food until settled
   - Observe baseline behavior before starting training

2. **"Get Under" Command**
   - Use as primary cue for settling under chair/table
   - Deliver first treat immediately upon successful positioning
   - Pair with calm verbal praise (low energy)

3. **Initial Treat Schedule: Continuous Reinforcement (CR)**
   - Treat every 30-60 seconds for first 2-3 sessions at each location
   - Reward: calm posture, relaxed breathing, quiet behavior
   - Mark and reward any self-correction (e.g., dog repositions quietly)

4. **Gradual Transition to Fixed Interval (FI)**
   - After 2-3 successful sessions, extend to 90-second intervals
   - Then to 2-minute intervals
   - Only progress when calm behavior is maintained without prompting

### Phase 2: Variable Interval Training (Weeks 3-4)

**Objective:** Build resilience to unpredictable reward timing while maintaining behavior.

**Why Variable Intervals?**
- Prevents "bonus seeking" behavior (dog working harder only right before expected treat)
- Creates steady, reliable response pattern
- More closely mimics real-world conditions

**Implementation:**
- Randomize treat timing: 1 min, 3 min, 2 min, 4 min (average ~2.5 min)
- Use a timer with vibration alert (set to random intervals) to keep yourself honest
- Mix in "life rewards": brief sniff breaks, verbal praise, continued petting

## 5. Progression Guidelines

### Extending Treat Intervals — The Method

Based on AKC's reinforcement schedule guidance:

**Step-by-Step Progression:**
1. **Start:** Every 30 seconds (continuous)
2. **Week 1:** Every 60-90 seconds
3. **Week 2:** Every 2 minutes (fixed interval)
4. **Week 3:** Variable interval 1-3 minutes (random)
5. **Week 4:** Variable interval 2-4 minutes (random)

**When to NOT Progress:**
- If Remi shows stress signals (pacing, panting, whining)
- If success rate drops below 80%
- At the first sign of frustration

### Differential Reinforcement

Only reward the **best** calm behavior:
- Tightest settle
- Quickest response to "get under"
- Longest sustained calm

But introduce this gradually—after Remi understands intermittent rewards.

## 6. Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Problem: Remi gets up frequently
**Solutions:**
- Use a leash to physically prevent getting up initially
- Practice "settle" command at home first
- Reduce session duration, increase treat frequency

### Problem: Barking/whining at familiar cafe
**Solutions:**
- Return to shorter intervals (go back to CR)
- Move to quieter time of day
- Practice "quiet" command separately before next session

### Problem: Working harder right before treat expection
**Solutions:**
- This indicates FI schedule pattern—switch to Variable Interval
- Add randomness to your timing
- Use variable duration holds before treats
