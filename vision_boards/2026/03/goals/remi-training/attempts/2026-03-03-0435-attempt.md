## 2. SESSION PROTOCOL

### 2.1 Pre-Session Checklist

**Equipment Needed:**
- [ ] High-value treats (small, soft, aromatic — e.g., chicken, cheese, freeze-dried liver)
- [ ] Treat pouch accessible on body
- [ ] Water bowl ( collapsible)
- [ ] Mat/bed (optional but recommended for consistent "place" association)
- [ ] Leash
- [ ] Data tracking notebook or digital device

**Pre-Visit Preparation:**
- [ ] Feed Remi a moderate meal 2-3 hours before session (hungry enough for treats, not ravenous)
- [ ] Ensure adequate pre-session exercise (15-20 min walk) to take edge off
- [ ] Review previous session's data to know current treat interval baseline

### 2.2 Arrival Protocol

1. **Walk past cafe** if Remi is excited — calm arrival sets tone
2. **Enter calmly** — handler models calm energy
3. **Select seating** — optimal spots:
   - Corner or wall on one side (reduces approach triggers)
   - Away from high-traffic pathways
   - Away from doors/kitchen sounds if possible
4. **Initial settle** — 2-3 minutes of silence before ordering

### 2.3 The "Get Under" Command Sequence

```
STEP 1: Cue "get under" while pointing/gesturing under table
STEP 2: Remi moves under chair/table
STEP 3: Mark with "yes" or clicker the MOMENT Remi is settled
STEP 4: Deliver treat directly to Remi (no waiting for sit/down — just calm position)
STEP 5: Wait [current interval] seconds
STEP 6: If Remi remains calm → treat + verbal praise ("good settle")
STEP 7: If Remi shifts/stands → wait for re-settle before treating
```

### 2.4 Body Language Indicators of Calm

**Calm Signals to Reinforce:**
- Soft eyes, blinking
- Relaxed ears (not pinned forward)
- Slow breathing
- Lying down or sitting with weight settled
- Gentle tail (neutral or slow wag)
- Paws relaxed, not pawing or gripping

**Pre-Correction Signals (before disruption):**
- Ears go forward (alert/stressed)
- Weight shifts forward
- Starts to lift head repeatedly
- Whining beginning
- Pacing or standing

**Response:** If pre-correction signals appear → redirect with gentle "settle" or "under" recall + immediate treat BEFORE barking/disruption occurs.

## 3. TREAT INTERVAL EXTENSION PROTOCEL

### 3.1 Variable Reinforcement Foundation

Research supports using **variable interval reinforcement** to build reliable behavior without constant treats. This means treats come at unpredictable intervals within a timeframe, not on a fixed schedule.

### 3.2 Interval Extension Timeline

| Phase | Treat Interval | Session Length | Criteria to Advance |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **A: Foundation** | Every 5-10 seconds | 10-15 min | 80%+ calm duration |
| **B: Building** | Every 15-20 seconds | 15-20 min | 80%+ calm duration |
| **C: Extending** | Every 30 seconds | 20-25 min | 80%+ calm duration |
| **D: Long** | Every 45-60 seconds | 25-30 min | 80%+ calm duration |
| **E: Maintenance** | Variable (30-90 sec) | 30+ min | Reliable behavior |

### 3.3 Extension Rules

**The 80% Rule:** Only increase interval when Remi has been calm for 80%+ of the session for 3 consecutive sessions at the current interval.

**The "Reset" Rule:** If a disruption (barking, standing, whining) occurs:
- Return to previous interval for that session
- Do NOT treat during or immediately after disruption
- Wait 10-15 seconds of calm before resuming treats at reduced frequency

**Never Skip More Than 2X:** Even at advanced intervals, do not go longer than 2X the current interval between treats (e.g., at 30-second interval, never exceed 60 seconds without reinforcement during learning phase).

### 3.4 Progressive Challenge Additions

As intervals extend, add controlled distractions:
1. Other patrons walking by (close pass → farther pass)
2. Server approaching table
3. Door opening/closing
4. Background music volume changes
5. Other dogs passing by (through window or at distance)

## 5. FAMILIAR CAFE SELECTION CRITERIA

### 5.1 Ideal Familiar Cafe Characteristics

For building consistent behavior, prioritize cafes that offer:

1. **Consistent staff** — Same baristas/staff means predictable greetings
2. **Regular schedule** — Can visit at consistent times to build routine
3. **Dog-friendly policy** — Legitimate welcome, not tolerance
4. **Seating configuration** — Tables with adequate under-space
5. **Moderate traffic** — Not overwhelming but not dead-quiet
6. **Comfortable atmosphere** — Not high-energy/party atmosphere

### 5.2 Recommended Familiar Cafe Progression

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2):** Visit 2-3 different cafes 1-2x each to establish which become "familiar"
**Phase 2 (Week 2-3):** Return to same cafe 3-4 times — build consistency
**Phase 3 (Week 3-4):** Test extension in familiar location, begin introducing mild variations

## 6. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

### 6.1 Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Won't go under table | Uncomfortable/lacks value | Practice "get under" at home first; pair with mat |
| Gets up constantly | Interval too long; not enough value | Shorten interval; upgrade treat value |
| Barks at other dogs | Pre-correction missed | Watch for head-lift, redirect BEFORE bark |
| Lies down but fussy | Undersocialized to environment | More sessions at lower-distraction times |
| Settles only when treating | Extinction burst / variable schedule confusion | Review interval; ensure some treats come when quiet, not just moving |

### 6.2 Recovery Protocol After Disruption

```
DISRUPTION OCCURS
      ↓
Do NOT make eye contact or talk to Remi (this rewards the behavior)
      ↓
Wait 5 seconds of silence/calm
      ↓
Say "settle" or "under" in calm voice
      ↓
When Remi complies → treat
      ↓
Resume at reduced interval (1 step back in extension timeline)
```
