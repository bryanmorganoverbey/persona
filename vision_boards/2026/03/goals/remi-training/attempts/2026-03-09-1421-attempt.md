## Part 1: Training Protocol Overview

### The "Get Under" Command Method

Based on research from veterinary professionals and dog training experts, here's the optimized protocol for Remi's cafe training:

**Pre-Session Preparation:**
1. **Exercise First** — Take Remi for a 30-60 minute walk before the cafe session. A tired dog is a well-behaved dog.
2. **Bring the Right Supplies:**
   - High-value treats (smaller, softer treats for frequent rewards)
   - A familiar mat or bed sheet for under the table
   - Portable water bowl
   - Chew toys or puzzle toys for distraction
   - Leash (short, 4 feet max)

**The Training Session Flow:**
| Phase | Action | Timing |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Arrive, get settled, let Remi sniff | 2-3 min |
| 2 | Issue "get under" command | Immediate |
| 3 | Initial treat + settle reward | Every 30 sec initially |
| 4 | Gradually extend intervals | See schedule below |
| 5 | End on positive note | Before Remi gets restless |

## Part 2: Treat Interval Progression Schedule

### Science-Backed Approach

According to the American Kennel Club, once a dog understands a behavior, transitioning from **continuous reinforcement** (CR) to **intermittent reinforcement** creates more resilient, reliable behavior. Here's how to progress:

### Recommended Progression for Familiar Cafes

| Level | Treat Interval | Session Duration | Criteria to Advance |
|-------|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Every 30 seconds | 15-20 min | Calm for full session |
| 2 | Every 60 seconds | 20-25 min | Calm for full session |
| 3 | Every 90 seconds | 25-30 min | Calm for full session |
| 4 | Every 2 minutes | 30-35 min | Calm for full session |
| 5 | Variable (1-3 min avg) | 35-45 min | Reliable in 3+ sessions |
| 6 | Variable (2-4 min avg) | 45-60 min | Mastered |

### Key Principles:
- **Variable interval rewards** (unpredictable timing) create the most steady, reliable behavior
- Always return to more frequent rewards if Remi shows regression
- Never advance two levels in one session
- Use "life rewards" occasionally (sniff breaks, praise, petting)

## Part 4: Success Criteria for "Consistent Calm Behavior"

Based on research, Remi has achieved **consistent calm behavior in familiar cafes** when:

### Behavioral Milestones (Checklist)
- [ ] Remains under table/chair for 30+ minutes without treats at >2 min intervals
- [ ] No barking or whining for entire session
- [ ] Settles voluntarily (lies down) without repeated commands
- [ ] Shows no stress signals (pacing, lip licking, panting)
- [ ] Recovers quickly if startled (servers, other dogs)
- [ ] Performs reliably across 3+ different familiar cafe locations

### Quantitative Targets
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Minimum calm duration | 30 minutes |
| Treat-free intervals | 2+ minutes |
| Sessions to consistency | 8-12 sessions |

## Part 5: Troubleshooting Common Issues

### If Remi Struggles:

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Won't stay under table | Practice at home first with table/chair |
| Barks at other dogs/people | Increase distance, use barrier, return to shorter intervals |
| Begs for food | Only reward when in down-stay, ignore begging |
| Anxious/restless | Shorten session, increase exercise before next session |
| Loses focus | Higher value treats, more frequent rewards initially |

## Part 6: Transitioning to New Environments

Once Remi achieves consistency in familiar cafes, use this protocol for new locations:

1. **Scout first** — Visit new cafe without Remi to assess noise level, foot traffic
2. **Start fresh** — Treat intervals reset to Level 1 in new environment
3. **Choose quiet times** — Avoid peak hours for initial sessions
4. **Pick strategic seating** — Corner booth, away from doors/kitchen
5. **Bring comfort item** — Familiar mat or blanket
6. **Lower expectations** — Accept shorter initial sessions in new environments
