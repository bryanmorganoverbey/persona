### 1. Core Training Framework: The Three Ds Applied to Cafe Training

The AKC's Three Ds framework provides the foundation for systematic progress:

| D | Definition | Application to Cafe Training |
|---|------------|------------------------------|
| **Duration** | Length of time maintaining calm behavior | Start with 1-2 seconds, build incrementally to 30+ minutes |
| **Distance** | How far you are from Remi | Start beside chair, progress to ordering at counter |
| **Distraction** | Environmental stimuli | Progress from quiet corners → familiar sounds → busy periods |

**Key Principle:** Only work on ONE "D" at a time, and always add distractions LAST.

### 2. Progressive Treat Interval Schedule

Based on research from dog training best practices, here's the recommended schedule for extending treat intervals:

#### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)
- **Interval:** Every 5-10 seconds
- **Treat size:** Small (pea-sized)
- **Criteria:** Remi must be calm and settled under chair

#### Phase 2: Building Duration (Weeks 3-4)
- **Interval:** Every 15-30 seconds
- **Treat size:** Small to medium
- **Criteria:** Increase only after 90%+ success rate at current interval

#### Phase 3: Extending (Weeks 5-6)
- **Interval:** Every 45-60 seconds
- **Treat size:** Medium
- **Criteria:** Random reinforcement (sometimes wait 90 seconds)

#### Phase 4: Maintenance (Weeks 7+)
- **Interval:** Every 2-5 minutes (variable)
- **Treat size:** Vary between small and high-value
- **Criteria:** Use unpredictable schedule to maintain alertness

**Critical Note:** According to AKC, treating throughout the duration (rather than just at the end) helps reinforce that staying in position = continued rewards. Pop tiny treats at intervals rather than one large treat at the end.

### 3. Cafe-Specific Training Protocol

#### Pre-Visit Preparation
- [ ] Exercise Remi before cafe visit (15-20 minutes of active play/walk)
- [ ] Bring: treats (high-value for cafe environment), water, portable mat (optional)
- [ ] Choose off-peak hours initially for familiar cafes
- [ ] Arrive with Remi in calm state, not overly excited

#### The "Get Under" Command Protocol
```
1. Enter cafe → Find seating → Direct Remi to spot under table/chair
2. Give "get under" command → Wait for Remi to settle
3. Initial treat: Reward immediately for getting under
4. Calm assessment: Wait 3-5 seconds to assess Remi's settling
5. If calm: Mark with "yes" or clicker → Treat
6. Begin interval schedule based on current training phase
7. Release with specific cue (e.g., "break" or "all done")
```

#### Environmental Grading Scale
| Level | Environment | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Familiar cafe, quiet time | Tuesday morning, 2 other patrons |
| 2 | Familiar cafe, moderate activity | Saturday afternoon |
| 3 | Familiar cafe, busy/popular time | Weekend brunch rush |
| 4 | New cafe, quiet time | Unfamiliar location |
| 5 | New cafe, busy time | Grand opening, happy hour |

**Progress Rule:** Only advance one level when 90%+ success achieved at current level.

### 6. Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Won't settle under chair | Too excited, not tired enough | Exercise before session; start with mat training at home |
| Gets up repeatedly | Distractions too high | Return to easier environment; increase treat frequency |
| Barks at waitstaff | Reactivity to movement | Desensitize with distance; treat BEFORE reaction threshold |
| Loses focus | Treats not valuable enough | Increase treat value; reduce session length |
| Anticipates release | Pattern recognition | Use unpredictable release cues; vary session length |

### 7. Mat Training Supplement

Research from Karen Overall's Relaxation Protocol (cited in Journey Dog Training) suggests using a portable mat as a "happy place" cue. For cafe training:

- **Bring a familiar mat/towel** that Remi associates with calm behavior
- **Place mat under table** as Remi's designated spot
- **Use "place" or "mat" as additional cue** alongside "get under"
- **Mat becomes portable trigger** for calm behavior in new environments

**Mat Training Progression:**
1. Train at home first (15-day protocol recommended)
2. Bring mat to quiet cafe corner
3. Gradually increase distractions while on mat
4. Eventually fade mat usage as "get under" becomes reliable
