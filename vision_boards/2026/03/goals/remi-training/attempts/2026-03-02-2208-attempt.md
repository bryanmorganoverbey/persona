## 2. Treat Interval Progression Guide

Based on variable reinforcement research, here's the recommended schedule:

### Phase 1: Continuous Reinforcement (Week 1-2)
- **Interval:** Every 30-60 seconds
- **Rule:** Treat EVERY time Remi is calm and under the chair
- **Goal:** Build strong association between "get under" + calm = rewards

### Phase 2: Differential Reinforcement (Week 3-4)
- **Interval:** Every 60-90 seconds
- **Rule:** Grade responses - excellent calm = high-value treat; good = regular treat; restless = wait for settling
- **Goal:** Teach Remi that quality of behavior affects reward value

### Phase 3: Variable Intermittent Schedule (Week 5+)
- **Interval:** Random 90-180 seconds
- **Rule:** Like a slot machine - treats come unpredictably
- **Expected Result:** Remi continues calm behavior hoping for reward (doesn't know when it's coming)

### Treat Fading Guidelines
- **Never eliminate treats completely** - switch to variable schedule
- **Pair with real-life rewards:** Access to sniffing, attention, petting, freedom
- **Increase reinforcement in new/distracting environments**
- **Maintain reinforcement in familiar low-distraction environments**

## 4. Best Practices for Familiar Cafes

### Pre-Session Checklist
- [ ] High-value treats brought (chicken, cheese, freeze-dried liver)
- [ ] Water available
- [ ] Dog mat/bed familiar to Remi
- [ ] Timing: after exercise, not during high energy

### During Session
- **Entry:** Wait outside, let Remi observe, give "get under" command
- **Positioning:** Under table/chair facing you or toward wall (less visual distraction)
- **First 5 minutes:** Higher treat frequency to establish calm
- **Body language cues:** Relaxed ears, soft eyes, lying down = treat
- **If restless:** Wait for settling before treating (don't reward agitation)

### Success Indicators
- Remi settles within 30 seconds of "get under"
- Remains calm with people walking by
- Doesn't fixate on other dogs/people
- Voluntary settling without command
- Tail wagging (not frantic)

### Troubleshooting
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Won't go under | Lure with treat, practice at home first |
| Restless/standing | Wait for sit/down, then reward |
| Barking at staff | Treat BEFORE they approach |
| Staring at distractions | Redirect attention, treat when eye contact returns |
| Getting up constantly | Shorter intervals, more movement back under |

## 6. Quick Reference Card

**"Get Under" Command Training**
1. Say command once
2. Lure under chair with treat if needed
3. Mark/click or say "yes" when all 4 paws under
4. Treat
5. Repeat

**Rule of Thumb:**
- New environment = treat every 30 seconds
- Familiar environment = treat every 60-90 seconds
- Mastered environment = variable 2-5 minutes

**Real-Life Rewards to Mix In:**
- Release to sniff (when calm)
- Verbal praise ("good settle")
- Physical affection
- Opportunity to watch people
- Play with toy after session
