# Extending Treat Intervals for Remi's Public Behavior Training
## A Progressive Framework for Reducing Treat Frequency While Maintaining Calm Behavior

### Understanding the Science: Reinforcement Schedules

The key to successfully extending treat intervals lies in understanding **variable interval (VI) schedules** — the most effective approach for maintaining duration-based behaviors like "stay calm under the table."

**Key Principles:**
- **Continuous Reinforcement (CR):** Give a treat every time — best for initially teaching the behavior
- **Variable Interval (VI):** Reward after unpredictable amounts of time — produces steady, reliable responses
- **Behaviors on intermittent schedules are more resistant to extinction** — meaning Remi will maintain calm behavior even when treats aren't guaranteed

**Why Variable Works Better Than Fixed:**
- With fixed intervals (e.g., every 30 seconds), dogs learn the pattern and only increase behavior right before reward time
- Variable schedules keep dogs engaged and attentive because they never know when the next treat is coming
- This produces steady, consistent calm behavior throughout the session

---

### Phase Progression Framework

Use this structured approach to extend intervals while maintaining reliability:

#### Phase 1: Baseline (Current State)
- **Interval:** Every 15-30 seconds
- **Setting:** Familiar cafes where Remi already behaves well
- **Criteria:** Remi is calm, quiet, and settled under the table for at least 90% of session
- **Duration:** 2-3 sessions at this level before advancing

#### Phase 2: Initial Extension
- **Interval:** Every 45-60 seconds (1.5x - 2x baseline)
- **Pattern:** Use variable timing — not predictable
- **Example sequence:** 45s → 30s → 60s → 50s → 70s → 40s (average ~50s)
- **What to watch for:** Any increase inalertness, pacing, or vocalization
- **Criteria:** Maintain calm for 2+ consecutive sessions before advancing

#### Phase 3: Moderate Extension
- **Interval:** Every 90-120 seconds (2x - 4x baseline)
- **Pattern:** Continue variable intervals
- **Example sequence:** 80s → 120s → 90s → 150s → 100s (average ~110s)
- **Introduce "life rewards":** Mix in non-food rewards occasionally (verbal praise, calm pet, opportunity to sniff)
- **Criteria:** Reliable calm in both familiar AND new environments

#### Phase 4: Advanced Extension
- **Interval:** Every 2-5 minutes (4x - 10x baseline)
- **Pattern:** Highly variable
- **Session structure:** Treat at start, then 2-3 times during a 30-45 minute cafe session
- **Maintenance:** Mix of treats, praise, and environmental rewards
- **Goal:** Remi remains calm for entire session with minimal treats

### Practical Implementation Guide

#### Session Structure Example (Familiar Cafe, 30-minute session)

**Early Phase (first 5 minutes):**
- Give "get under" command
- Treat immediately for compliance
- Treat every 30 seconds for first 5 minutes

**Middle Phase (minutes 5-20):**
- Begin extending to 45-60 second intervals
- Use variable pattern: 45s → 30s → 60s → 50s → 70s → 45s

**Late Phase (minutes 20-30):**
- Reduce to 1-2 treats total
- Use life rewards (calm "good boy," brief attention)
- Reinforce settling behavior

### Signs of Successful Extension

**Positive indicators:**
- Remi settles quickly after "get under"
- Remains calm between treats without looking expectant
-ignores distractions (servers, other customers, noise)
- Sleeps or rests quietly under table
- Shows no signs of stress or frustration

**Warning signs (slow down or pause extension):**
- Frequent looking up at you or toward food
- Restlessness, pacing, or inability to settle
- Whining, panting, or lip licking
- Breaking "under" position to check in with you
- Hyperactivity or alertness when you reach for treats

### Key Strategies for Success

1. **Use tiny treats** — reduce treat size, not number of rewards, to manage calorie intake

2. **Be unpredictable** — never let Remi anticipate exactly when the next treat is coming

3. **Only reward calm** — treat only when Remi is settled; withholding treats during agitation teaches that calm = rewards

4. **Never skip sessions** — consistent practice is essential; variable schedules only work with regular training

5. **Mix reward types** — add verbal praise, chin scratches, and environmental rewards (brief sniff breaks) to reduce treat dependency

6. **Progress gradually** — only increase interval by 25-50% at a time; ensure 80%+ success rate before extending further

7. **Watch for regressions** — high-distraction environments may require returning to shorter intervals temporarily

---

### Environment-Specific Recommendations

**Familiar Cafes (low stress):**
- Push extension faster — Remi is comfortable and confident
- Can achieve Phase 3-4 intervals here

**New Restaurants (medium stress):**
- Start with shorter intervals than usual for first 1-3 visits
- Gradually normalize intervals as Remi gains confidence
- May need to reset to Phase 1-2 for each new location

**High-Distraction Events:**
- Maintain closer intervals (30-60 seconds)
- Consider reducing expectations to just calm, not necessarily fully settled
- Use higher-value treats when treating less frequently

---

### Troubleshooting

**Problem: Remi gets antsy when intervals extend**
- Solution: You're extending too fast. Return to shorter intervals and advance more slowly
- Add more "life rewards" (calm praise) between treats

**Problem: Remi stops responding to "get under"**
- Solution: Increase reinforcement rate temporarily to rebuild reliability before trying again

**Problem: Remi only stays calm when watching for treats**
- Solution: You're being too predictable. Make intervals more variable

**Problem: Regression in new environments**
- Solution: Normal and expected — treat new environments like starting over, but progress faster this time
