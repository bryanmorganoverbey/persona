### 🎯 Treat Interval Progression Guide

Based on canine reinforcement science, use this systematic approach:

| Stage | Interval | Method | When to Advance |
|-------|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **1. Continuous** | Every 2-3 seconds | Reward every calm moment | After 3 successful sessions |
| **2. Fixed Interval** | Every 5 seconds | Predictable timing | Dog remains calm between rewards |
| **3. Fixed Interval** | Every 10 seconds | Count in head | No regression in behavior |
| **4. Variable Interval** | 10-20 seconds avg | Randomize timing | Reliable for 2+ weeks |
| **5. Variable Interval** | 20-40 seconds avg | Less frequent | Solid reliability achieved |
| **6. Maintenance** | Random/sparse | Life rewards + occasional treats | Behavior is bulletproof |

**Key Principle:** Only advance to the next interval level when Remi is consistently successful at the current level. If regression occurs, drop back one level.

### ☕ Familiar Cafe Best Practices

#### Selecting Ideal Training Cafes
1. **Start with low-traffic times:** Weekday mornings or early afternoons
2. **Choose familiar staff:** Cafes where team knows Remi
3. **Consistent seating:** Request same table when possible
4. **Moderate distraction level:** Not too busy, but enough activity to generalize

#### Pre-Session Checklist
- [ ] Water bowl placed under table
- [ ] High-value treats ready (cheese, chicken, freeze-dried)
- [ ] Leash handler ready
- [ ] Distraction level assessed before entering
- [ ] Exit strategy planned if needed

#### During Session
1. **Enter calm** — Wait for Remi to settle before treats begin
2. **Command "get under"** — Clear, consistent cue
3. **Initial treats** — Higher frequency at start (every 2-3 sec for first 2 minutes)
4. **Gradual reduction** — Slow treat delivery once settled
5. **Watch for signals** — Lying down, head on paws = truly calm
6. **Random rewards** — Vary timing to maintain engagement

#### Success Indicators
- ✅ Remi goes under without hesitation when commanded
- ✅ Lies down voluntarily under table
- ✅ Head rests on paws (not alert/standing)
- ✅ Eyes soft/not staring at you or food
- ✅ Body language relaxed (no tension)
- ✅ No vocalization for entire session

### 🐕 Signs of True Relaxation vs. Alert State

| Relaxed (Reward!) | Alert (Not Yet Ready) |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Head on paws | Standing/weight forward |
| Eyes half-closed or soft | Wide eyes, staring |
| Body lying flat | Tense body, up on elbows |
| Slow breathing | Rapid breathing/panting |
| Taking treats gently | Snatching/ gulping |
| Ignoring environmental sounds | Head turning to sounds |
