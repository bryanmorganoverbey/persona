# Remi Public Behavior Training
## Task: Consistent Calm Behavior in Familiar Cafes

### Training Environment Selection

**Priority: Familiar Cafes First**
Training should begin in known, predictable environments before progressing to new locations. This follows the principle of building reliability in low-distraction settings before introducing novel stimuli.

**Ideal Familiar Cafe Characteristics:**
- Consistent staff who know Remi
- Regular visit schedule (same days/times when possible)
- Calm atmosphere with moderate foot traffic
- Adequate space under tables/chairs
- Pet-friendly policy established

---

### "Get Under" Command Protocol

**Command:** "Get under"
**Target Behavior:** Remi voluntarily goes under chair/table and lies down calmly

**Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)**
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Treat Frequency | Every 5-10 seconds |
| Session Duration | 10-15 minutes max |
| Treat Delivery | High-value treats, small pieces |
| Success Rate Target | 90%+ compliance |

**Phase 2: Building Duration (Weeks 3-4)**
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Treat Frequency | Every 15-30 seconds |
| Session Duration | 15-20 minutes |
| Introduce Verbal Praise | Combined with occasional treats |
| Success Rate Target | 85%+ compliance |

**Phase 3: Extending Intervals (Weeks 5-6)**
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Treat Frequency | Every 45-60 seconds |
| Session Duration | 20-30 minutes |
| Variable Rewards | Random intervals, not predictable |
| Success Rate Target | 80%+ compliance |

---

### Treat Interval Extension Framework

Following the **Variable Intervals Schedule of Reinforcement** (the "slot machine" method), treats are given randomly rather than at predictable intervals. This builds higher motivation and reliability.

**Progressive Interval Protocol:**

```
Week 1-2:  Every 5 seconds (continuous)
Week 3:    Every 10 seconds
Week 4:    Every 20 seconds
Week 5:    Every 30 seconds
Week 6:    Every 45 seconds
Week 7:    Every 60 seconds (variable)
Week 8+:   Variable 30-90 seconds (randomized)
```

**Key Principle:** Never let Remi predict exactly when the next treat comes. This prevents "strike" behavior where the dog refuses to work without a guaranteed reward.

**Real-Life Rewards Integration:**
As training progresses, incorporate non-food rewards:
- Verbal praise ("Good boy, Remi!")
- Physical affection (calm petting)
- Permission to sniff/explore after session
- Access to water

### Pre-Session Preparation Checklist

Before arriving at familiar cafe:

- [ ] Exercise completed (15-30 min minimum)
- [ ] Bathroom break taken
- [ ] High-value treats packed
- [ ] Water available
- [ ] "Get under" command practiced at home
- [ ] Vet check: no underlying discomfort causing restlessness

**Pro Tip:** A tired dog is a calm dog. Schedule cafe visits after exercise sessions.

---

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Won't settle under table | Too excited/anxious | Extend pre-visit exercise; start with shorter sessions |
| Barking at staff/other dogs | Distraction level too high | Move to quieter area; increase distance from triggers |
| Won't take treats | Not hungry or over-stimulated | Reduce session length; use higher value treats |
| Paws on ground/restless | Uncomfortable position | Bring familiar mat/bed; try different table |
| Success in one cafe, not another | Context-specific learning | Label each cafe as separate training environment |

---

### Transition to New Environments

Once Remi demonstrates consistent calm behavior in 3+ familiar cafes:

1. **New Cafe Protocol:**
   - Begin with familiar treat interval (shorter at first)
   - Allow 2-3 sessions at new location before extending intervals
   - Treat more frequently in new environment initially
   - Return to familiar cafe for successful "confidence building"

2. **Generalization Training:**
   - Practice "get under" in various contexts (different chairs, floor types, noise levels)
   - This prevents context-specific learning
