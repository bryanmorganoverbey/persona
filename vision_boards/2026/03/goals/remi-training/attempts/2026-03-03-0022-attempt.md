### Phase 1: Establishing Consistent Calm Behavior (Weeks 1-2)

**Objective:** Achieve reliable, calm behavior in 2-3 familiar cafes with known distraction levels

#### Pre-Session Checklist

| Item | Status |
|------|--------|
| High-value treats (cheese, chicken, special treats) | ☐ |

| Water bowl | ☐ |

| Treat pouch accessible | ☐ |
| Timer/stopwatch for tracking | ☐ |

#### Session Protocol: Familiar Cafes

1. **Arrival (0-2 minutes)**
   - Arrive during off-peak hours (typically 2-4 PM)
   - Request a quieter table section if possible
   - Set up Remi's mat/blanket under the table
   - Attach leash to chair leg for safety
   - Allow 1-2 minutes for initial sniffing and settling

2. **Command Sequence**
   - Use "get under" cue clearly
   - Reward immediately when Remi settles under the table
   - Initial treat: within first 5 seconds of settling

3. **Initial Treat Schedule (Continuous Reinforcement)**
   ```
   First 3-5 sessions: Treat every 5-10 seconds of calm behavior
   - Reward any calm posture (lying down, sitting quietly)
   - Treat at consistent intervals while Remi remains settled
   - If Remi moves or gets up, reset and start timing again
   ```

4. **Success Criteria per Session**
   - [ ] Remains under table for minimum 5 minutes without disruption
   - [ ] No barking or whining
   - [ ] Settles within 30 seconds of "get under" command
   - [ ] Recovers quickly if startled by noise/people

### Phase 2: Extending Treat Intervals (Weeks 3-4)

**Objective:** Transition from continuous to intermittent reinforcement while maintaining behavior

#### The Science: Why Intermittent Reinforcement Works

According to the American Kennel Club, intermittent (partial) reinforcement schedules are more effective for maintaining behavior long-term than continuous reinforcement. Once a dog understands what you want, intermittent rewards create more resilient behaviors that don't fade when treats become less predictable.

#### Progressive Interval Extension Protocol

| Stage | Interval Between Treats | Duration Goal | Notes |
|-------|------------------------|---------------|-------|
| 1 | Every 10-15 seconds | 5 minutes | Build confidence first |
| 2 | Every 20-30 seconds | 8 minutes | Watch for restlessness |
| 3 | Every 45-60 seconds | 12 minutes | Randomize timing slightly |
| 4 | Every 60-90 seconds | 15 minutes | Variable intervals |
| 5 | Every 90-120 seconds | 20+ minutes | Introduce life rewards |

**Key Principle:** Use **variable interval reinforcement** - reward after unpredictable time intervals. This keeps Remi engaged and guessing, preventing the "paycheck" effect where dogs only work when they see a treat coming.

#### Implementation Tips

- **Never reward while Remi is moving** - wait for settled posture
- **Use "jackpot" rewards** (multiple treats in a row) randomly to reinforce the behavior
- **Pair treats with life rewards** - after a successful session, reward with a short walk or sniff session outside
- **Never skip two consecutive intervals** - this can cause extinction bursts

### Phase 4: Troubleshooting Common Issues

#### Problem: Restlessness / Can't Settle

**Causes:**
- Too many distractions at current cafe
- Need higher value treats
- Session too long for current stamina
- Needs more exercise before training session

**Solutions:**
- Return to easier location temporarily
- Increase treat value (try chicken, cheese, or hot dog pieces)
- Shorten sessions to 10-15 minutes max
- Take Remi for a 20-minute walk before cafe training

#### Problem: Barking at Other Dogs/People

**Solutions:**
- Increase distance from triggers
- Use higher value treats for "look at me" disengagement
- Practice "get under" command at home with distractions first
- Consider bark-calming command if trained

#### Problem: Gets Up Constantly

**Solutions:**
- Return to shorter intervals temporarily
- Use a mat that marks the "place" clearly
- Keep leash gently attached to prevent wandering
- Practice "settle" command at home before cafe work

### Recommended Familiar Cafe Selection Criteria

For consistent progress, choose cafes that meet these criteria:

| Factor | Ideal | Avoid |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Noise level** | Moderate background music | Live music, loud TVs |
| **Foot traffic** | Low to moderate | High foot traffic areas |
| **Dog policy** | Pet-friendly, expect dogs | Uncomfortable with dogs |
| **Table setup** | Spacious under-table area | Cramped seating |
| **Crowd patterns** | Predictable quiet times | Inconsistent rush hours |
| **Staff** | Welcoming, ignore dog | Hostile or distracting |

**Suggested Peak Training Times:**
- Weekdays: 2:00 - 4:30 PM
- Weekends: Early morning (8-10 AM) or mid-afternoon
