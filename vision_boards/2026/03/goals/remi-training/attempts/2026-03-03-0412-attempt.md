### 2. Reinforcement Schedule Progression Guide

Based on behavioral science research, this guide outlines how to gradually reduce treat frequency while maintaining calm behavior.

#### Phase 1: Continuous Reinforcement (Current - Week 1-2)
- **Schedule:** Treat for every correct response
- **Interval:** Every 30-60 seconds
- **When to use:** New environments, initial learning, after setbacks
- **Goal:** Build strong association between "get under" and positive outcomes

#### Phase 2: Fixed Interval Schedule (Week 3-4)
- **Schedule:** Treat after a fixed time period of calm behavior
- **Interval:** Every 90 seconds to 2 minutes
- **When to use:** When Remi reliably stays under without prompting
- **Implementation:** 
  - Start at 60 seconds, extend by 15-second increments
  - Only reward if Remi remains calm for the full interval
  - If Remi breaks calm, reset timer

#### Phase 3: Variable Interval Schedule (Week 5-6)
- **Schedule:** Treat after unpredictable time intervals
- **Interval range:** 1-4 minutes (randomized)
- **When to use:** Building reliability, preventing boredom
- **Why it works:** Dogs maintain steady behavior because they don't know when the reward comes
- **Tip:** Use a random timer or vary manually (e.g., 1 min, 3 min, 2 min, 4 min)

#### Phase 4: Intermittent Reinforcement (Week 7+)
- **Schedule:** Mix of treats, praise, and life rewards
- **Frequency:** Every 3-5 minutes of calm behavior
- **When to use:** Maintaining behavior in familiar environments
- **Life rewards to incorporate:**
  - Release to sniff around
  - Brief attention/pets
  - A piece of your food (if appropriate)
  - Verbal praise ("good settle")

#### Important Rules for Extending Intervals

1. **Never extend too fast.** If Remi breaks calm behavior, return to previous interval for 2-3 sessions before trying to extend again.
2. **Randomize timing.** Dogs are pattern detectors. Predictable intervals lead to break behavior right before treat time.
3. **Use a release cue.** "Okay" or "free" signals when the behavior period is truly over.
4. **Pair with environmental cues.** Same cafe = lower treats, new cafe = higher treats initially.

### 4. Cafe Training Best Practices

#### Pre-Session Preparation

- **Exercise first:** 30-60 minute walk/exercise before cafe visit
- **Timing:** Train after a meal (natural sleepy tendency)
- **Treats:** Bring high-value treats, pre-portioned in a pouch
- **Water:** Bring portable bowl, place under table
- **Backup:** Bring a chew toy or Kong as emergency distractor

#### During the Session

1. **Choose optimal seating:**
   - Corner booth or quiet area
   - Away from high traffic (door, kitchen)
   - Not facing other dogs or stimulating areas

2. **Leash management:**
   - Keep on 4-foot leash attached to chair/belt loop
   - Not attached to table
   - Allow enough slack to lie comfortably

3. **Command execution:**
   - Say "get under" once, firmly but calmly
   - Point to under the table
   - If needed, guide with leash
   - Mark success immediately with "yes" or "good"

4. **Treat delivery:**
   - Drop treats on floor near paws (not from above face)
   - Say "calm" or "settle" as Remi eats
   - Return hand to lap, wait for next interval

5. **Handling distractions:**
   - If Remi reacts, redirect with "get under" command
   - Do not reward during excitement
   - Wait for calm, then reward

#### Recognizing Warning Signs

Stop training and leave if Remi shows:
- Excessive panting or drooling
- Pacing despite correction
- Persistent whining/barking
- Attempts to move toward other tables
- Whale eye (showing whites of eyes)
- Ears pinned back

These indicate overwhelm. Better to end on a neutral note than force continued stress.

### 6. New Environment Testing Protocol

When testing Remi in unfamiliar restaurants:

1. **First visit (new location):**
   - Return to continuous reinforcement
   - Treat every 30-60 seconds
   - Expect more vigilance needed
   - Keep session short (15-20 minutes max)

2. **Second visit (new location):**
   - Move to fixed interval (90 seconds)
   - Observe if location memory helps

3. **Third visit onwards:**
   - Treat like familiar cafe if successful
   - If struggles, treat as setback and rebuild
