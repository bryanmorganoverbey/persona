## Training Protocol

### 1. Pre-Cafe Preparation

**Exercise Before Arrival (30-60 minutes)**
- Take Remi for a long walk or play session before the cafe
- A tired dog is a well-behaved dog
- Physical exercise depletes energy and reduces restlessness

**What to Bring**
- High-value treats (small, soft treats work best)
- Portable water bowl
- Comfort item (if Remi has a favorite toy or blanket)
- Leash (short, 4 feet max)
- Treat pouch for easy access

---

### 2. "Get Under" Command Protocol

**Step 1: Initial Command**
- Give the "get under" command as Remi approaches the chair/table
- Use a calm, firm voice
- Point under the table as a visual cue

**Step 2: Initial Positioning**
- Have Remi lie down under the table
- Ensure comfortable positioning (Remi should be able to fully lie down)
- Choose a corner spot away from high traffic

**Step 3: Treat Reinforcement Schedule**
| Phase | Treat Interval | Session Duration |
|-------|---------------|------------------|
| Week 1-2 | Every 30 seconds | 15-20 minutes |
| Week 3-4 | Every 60 seconds | 20-30 minutes |
| Week 5-6 | Every 90 seconds | 30-45 minutes |
| Week 7+ | Every 2-3 minutes | 45-60 minutes |

**Step 4: Criteria for Extending Intervals**
- Remi remains calm (no whining, barking, or fidgeting)
- Remi stays in position without prompting
- Remi responds to mild distractions

---

### 3. Extending Treat Intervals (Reducing Frequency)

**The 3-Second Rule**
- When Remi stops barking/making noise, wait 3 seconds before treating
- Gradually increase this to 5, 10, 15, and eventually 30+ seconds
- This teaches Remi that silence = reward

**Variable Reinforcement Schedule**
- Once behavior is reliable, switch from continuous to variable reinforcement
- Treat every 2nd or 3rd calm instance
- This creates stronger, more durable behavior

**Gradual Extension Protocol**
1. Start with 30-second intervals
2. Hold for 2 consecutive sessions with no issues
3. Increase interval by 15-30 seconds
4. Hold at new interval for 2-3 sessions
5. Continue until reaching target duration

---

### 4. Handling Distractions

**Common Cafe Distractions**
- Other dogs passing by
- Waiters/staff approaching
- Food smells
- People talking/loud noises
- Dropped items

**Training Approach**
- Start in low-distraction environments
- Gradually introduce higher distraction scenarios
- Use higher value treats for distraction-proofing
- If Remi reacts, redirect with a calm "settle" command

## Criteria for "Consistent Calm Behavior"

### Definition of Success
- [ ] Remi goes under table/chair within 10 seconds of command
- [ ] Remi remains calm for 30+ minutes without treats
- [ ] Remi does not bark or make noise at distractions
- [ ] Remi settles automatically after initial command
- [ ] Success rate of 80%+ across familiar cafes

### Behavior Indicators
**Calm Behavior:**
- Lying down with head on paws
- Relaxed body language (loose muscles)
- Steady breathing
- Eyes soft or partially closed
- No whining, barking, or fidgeting

**Warning Signs:**
- Pacing or standing up repeatedly
- Whining or soft vocalizations
- Staring at distractions
- Tense body posture
- Heavy panting (if not hot)

## Troubleshooting Guide

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Won't stay under table | Uncomfortable or too exposed | Practice at home first; use a mat |
| Barks at people/dogs | Over-stimulated | Increase distance from trigger; use higher value treats |
| Treat-motivated but won't calm down | Too excited | Longer pre-cafe exercise; practice "settle" command |
| Loses focus after a few minutes | Fatigue or boredom | Shorten sessions; add play breaks |
| Works at home, not in public | Generalization issue | Practice in multiple locations; more repetitions |
