---

### Quick Reference - Distraction Level Scale

- **1 - Very Quiet**: Almost empty, minimal movement, few people
- **2 - Low Traffic**: Some people, occasional movement, relaxed atmosphere
- **3 - Moderate**: Regular foot traffic, some noise, multiple people nearby
- **4 - Busy**: Frequent movement, ambient noise, several groups
- **5 - Very Busy**: Packed venue, loud, multiple distractions, high activity

### Quick Reference - Behavior Rating Scale

- **1 - Restless**: Constant movement, couldn't settle, reactive
- **2 - Unsettled**: Frequently shifted position, some whining
- **3 - Okay**: Settled but required frequent reinforcement
- **4 - Calm**: Good settling, occasional checks but relaxed overall
- **5 - Perfect**: Stayed under quietly the entire time, fully relaxed

### How to Use This Data

**Tracking Progress:**
1. Record every session consistently
2. Use the weekly summary to identify trends
3. Compare behavior ratings across different environment types
4. Note which locations/stimuli are most challenging

**Extending Treat Intervals:**
- When average behavior rating is 4+ for a week, increase treat interval by 1-2 minutes
- Reduce treats while maintaining behavior rating = progress indicator
- If behavior rating drops, temporarily decrease interval

**Making Data-Driven Decisions:**
- New environment + lower rating = expected, plan more reinforcement
- Familiar environment + high rating = opportunity to extend interval
- Consistent low ratings = investigate triggers, consider training adjustments

### Tips for Effective Tracking

1. **Be consistent** - Log immediately after each session while details are fresh
2. **Be honest** - Record actual behavior, not ideal behavior
3. **Track environment details** - Different locations have different distraction levels
4. **Note patterns** - Look for correlations between time of day, location type, and behavior
5. **Celebrate progress** - Use data to confirm improvements, not just identify issues
