## PART 1: TRAINING PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2) — Familiar Cafes Only

**Objective:** Build reliability in known environments with high-value reinforcement

**Session Structure:**
1. **Arrival** — Allow Remi to settle for 2-3 minutes before giving "get under" command
2. **Command** — Say "get under" clearly while pointing under table/chair
3. **Initial Treats** — Treat every 30-60 seconds for the first 10 minutes (reward calm posture)
4. **Extension** — Gradually stretch to 2-minute intervals if calm
5. **Release** — Use a consistent release word ("okay," "free," "all done")

**Success Criteria for Phase 1:**
- [ ] Remi goes under command within 5 seconds
- [ ] Remi remains quiet (no whining, barking, or pawing) for 10+ minutes
- [ ] Settles quickly after distractions (servers, other customers)

---

### Phase 2: Interval Extension (Weeks 3-4) — Familiar Cafes

**Objective:** Reduce treat frequency while maintaining calm behavior

**Progressive Interval Schedule:**

| Week | Target Interval Between Treats | Session Duration |
|------|-------------------------------|------------------|
| 3.1  | 3 minutes                     | 20 minutes       |
| 3.2  | 4 minutes                     | 25 minutes       |
| 4.1  | 5 minutes                     | 30 minutes       |
| 4.2  | 7 minutes                     | 35 minutes       |

**Key Principles:**
- **Variable Reinforcement:** Once behavior is established, vary the timing (sometimes 4 min, sometimes 6 min) to prevent predictability
- **"Limited Hold" Rule:** Only deliver treat if Remi is calm at the moment the interval ends — this prevents "marking" the exact moment
- **Mock Rewards:** Occasionally mark with "good" but don't treat every time (use ~70% reinforcement rate once established)

**Warning Signs of Regression:**
- Pawing or whining before treat time
- Rising from settled position repeatedly
- Excessive alertness/scanning environment

---

### Phase 3: Testing in New Environments (Week 5+)

**Procedure for Unfamiliar Cafes:**
1. Start with "easier" new environments (quiet times, smaller cafes)
2. Return to higher treat frequency initially (every 1-2 minutes)
3. Apply same interval extension protocol once settled
4. Track performance vs. familiar cafes in data log

## PART 3: QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE

### Command Checklist for Cafe Sessions

- [ ] **"Get Under"** — Point under table, wait for compliance
- [ ] **Settle** — Wait for lying down/relaxed posture before first treat
- [ ] **Quiet** — Confirm no noise before delivering treat
- [ ] **Eye Contact** (optional) — Brief check-in can earn reward
- [ ] **Release** — Clear verbal release, don't let Remi decide when

### Treat Delivery Tips

1. **Low & Slow** — Deliver treats on the floor, not in the air (prevents jumping)
2. **Quiet Praise** — Keep voice calm; excited praise = excited dog
3. **Hand Position** — Feed from hand closest to Remi, not reaching over
4. **High Value** — In distracting environments, use higher-value treats

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Won't go under | Fear of confined space | Practice "under" at home first with crate/mat |
| Gets up repeatedly | Treats too frequent | Wait longer between treats, require settling |
| Barks at people | Overstimulated | Move to quieter area, increase distance |
| Whines for treats | Anticipatory | Use variable intervals, withhold until quiet |
