## 2. Best Practices for the "Get Under" Command

### Command Execution
- **Verbal Cue**: "Get under" — clear, consistent command
- **Visual Cue**: Point under the chair/table
- **Hand Signal**: Open palm sweeping downward toward the under-area

### Training Sequence (from research)
1. **Phase 1 - Foundation** (home/low distraction)
   - Practice "get under" with familiar furniture
   - Reward immediately upon compliance
   - Use high-value treats initially

2. **Phase 2 - Generalization**
   - Practice at different tables/chairs
   - Vary the type of furniture
   - Introduce mild background noise

3. **Phase 3 - Public Settings**
   - Begin with familiar cafes (known to be dog-friendly)
   - Arrive during off-peak hours
   - Choose corner/quieter seating areas

### Equipment Recommendations
- Bring a designated mat/towel for Remi's "place"
- Use a treat pouch for quick access
- Keep water bowl accessible
- Bring high-value backup treats for high-distraction moments

## 3. Treat Interval Extension Framework

### Reinforcement Schedule Progression

Based on AKC research on reinforcement schedules:

| Stage | Schedule Type | Treat Frequency | Example |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|---------|
| Learning | Continuous (CR) | Every correct response | Every time Remi gets under |
| Early Practice | Fixed Ratio (FR) | Every 2-3 responses | Treat after every 2 sits under |
| Intermediate | Variable Ratio (VR) | Random intervals (avg every 3-5) | Unpredictable timing |
| Advanced | Variable Interval (VI) | Time-based, unpredictable | Every 30-90 seconds randomly |

### Recommended Progression for Familiar Cafes

**Week 1-2: High Reinforcement**
- Treat every 30 seconds of calm behavior
- 15-20 treats per session

**Week 3-4: Reducing Frequency**
- Treat every 60 seconds
- 10-12 treats per session

**Week 5-6: Variable Schedule**
- Treat at varying intervals (30-90 seconds)
- 6-8 treats per session

**Week 7+: Maintenance**
- Random reinforcement
- 3-5 treats per session with life rewards (release to sniff, praise)

### Key Principle
> "Variable reinforcement schedules create resistance to extinction" — but for reliability, use a mix of treats and life rewards (sniff breaks, verbal praise, access to look around).

## 4. Distraction Level Scaling

Use this scale to track and progressively increase difficulty:

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Quiet, familiar environment | Home, familiar room |
| 2 | Low traffic, few people | Quiet cafe, morning hours |
| 3 | Moderate activity | Weekend cafe, outdoor patio |
| 4 | High activity | Busy lunch rush, groups arriving |
| 5 | Maximum distraction | Peak hours, live music, events |

### Progression Rules
- Only advance to next level when 80%+ success at current level
- If success drops below 60%, return to previous level
- Always start new locations at Level 2 or 3

## 5. Success Criteria for "Consistent Calm Behavior"

### Definition of Success

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Session Duration | 30+ minutes |
| Calm Behavior Percentage | 90%+ |
| Vocalizations (barking/whining) | 0 |
| Unsolicited movement | Under 3 incidents |
| Treat interval | Variable, 60+ seconds average |

### Behavioral Indicators of Success
- [ ] Remi goes under table/chair within 3 seconds of command
- [ ] Remi settles within 30 seconds of getting under
- [ ] Remi remains in position when people walk by

## 6. New Environment Transition Checklist

Before testing in unfamiliar restaurants:

- [ ] Review location pet policy online
- [ ] Call ahead to confirm dog-friendly policy
- [ ] Visit during off-peak hours first
- [ ] Choose outdoor/patio seating if available
- [ ] Bring extra high-value treats
- [ ] Have backup plan (alternative location ready)
- [ ] Keep first sessions short (15-20 minutes)
- [ ] Set Remi up for success — don't rush the process

## 8. Quick Reference Card

### Before Leaving for Cafe
1. ✅ Exercise first (15-20 min walk or play)
2. ✅ Feed a small meal
3. ✅ Practice "get under" at home
4. ✅ Pack: treats, water, mat, cleanup supplies

### At the Cafe
1. Find ideal spot (quiet corner, under table)
2. Give "get under" command
3. Initial high-frequency treats (first 2-3 minutes)
4. Gradually extend intervals
5. Reward calm, ignore minor movements
6. Release for brief sniff breaks as reward

### Warning Signs (Pause Training)
- Excessive panting or drooling
- Pacing or inability to settle
- Vocalizing repeatedly
- Extreme alertness/jumping at sounds
