# Remi Public Behavior Training: Consistent Calm Behavior in Familiar Cafes

## Summary

This deliverable provides a comprehensive training guide for achieving consistent calm behavior from Remi in familiar cafe environments. Drawing from established dog training principles including the "Three Ds" of dog training (Duration, Distance, Distraction) and reinforcement schedule theory, this document outlines step-by-step protocols, data tracking frameworks, and progression strategies to systematically build Remi's ability to remain quietly under tables/chairs at cafes without barking or making noise.

---

## Findings / Results

### 1. Training Foundation: The "Get Under" Command

The core command "get under" should be trained using positive reinforcement following this sequence:

**Phase 1: Initial Learning (Home Environment)**
- Start in a quiet, distraction-free environment
- Use a comfortable spot under a chair or small table
- Lure Remi under with treats, saying "get under" as they move
- Reward immediately when fully under the furniture
- Practice 5-10 repetitions per session, 2-3 times daily

**Phase 2: Adding the Cue**
- Once Remi understands the movement, say "get under" BEFORE they move
- Wait for the behavior, then mark and reward
- Build reliability before adding any distractions

### 2. Understanding the Three Ds Applied to Cafe Training

Per the American Kennel Club's training methodology, all behaviors must be trained across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Application to Cafe Training | Progression Rate |
|-----------|------------------------------|------------------|
| **Duration** | How long Remi remains calm under the table | Build 1-2 seconds at a time |
| **Distance** | How far you are from Remi (initially at table, then stepping away) | Start with 0 distance, then 1-2 feet |
| **Distraction** | Environmental stimuli (servers, other dogs, sounds) | Introduce gradually after duration/distance are solid |

**Critical Principle**: Only work on ONE "D" at a time. When increasing difficulty in one area, reduce the others to set Remi up for success.

### 3. Reinforcement Schedule Strategy

Based on operant conditioning research, the following schedule progression applies:

**Continuous Reinforcement (Learning Phase)**
- Reward EVERY correct response during initial cafe training
- This builds the connection between behavior and consequence
- Use high-value treats in the beginning

**Fixed Interval Schedule (Building Duration)**
- After reliable basic behavior, switch to rewarding at set time intervals
- Example: Every 5 seconds of calm behavior gets a treat
- Gradually extend intervals as Remi demonstrates success

**Variable Interval Schedule (Maintaining Behavior)**
- Reward after random time intervals (average of target time)
- Example: 3s, then 7s, then 2s, then 8s = avg 5 seconds
- This creates the most resilient, reliable behavior
- Final goal: Variable schedule with 30-60+ second intervals

### 4. Familiar Cafe Training Protocol

**Pre-Visit Preparation**
- Choose 2-3 "familiar" cafes where Remi has already had positive experiences
- Visit off-peak hours initially (fewer distractions)
- Bring: High-value treats, water, mat (optional), leash
- Ensure Remi has had adequate exercise before training session

**Session Structure (Target: 20-30 minutes)**

| Segment | Duration | Focus |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Arrival & Settle | 5 min | Allow Remi to sniff, acclimate |
| Command Practice | 10-15 min | "Get under" with treat rewards |
| Duration Building | 5-10 min | Extend time between treats |
| Cool Down | 2-3 min | Release and praise |

**Treat Administration Protocol**
- Deliver treats directly to Remi under the table (avoid bending awkwardly)
- Treat WHILE Remi is in position, not after they move
- Use tiny treats to avoid overfeeding during long sessions
- Consider using a treat pouch for quick access

### 6. Progression Milestones

**Milestone 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)**
- [ ] Reliable "get under" in quiet home environment
- [ ] Remi goes under table within 3 seconds of command
- [ ] Can hold position for 30+ seconds with frequent treats

**Milestone 2: Familiar Cafe Introduction (Weeks 2-3)**
- [ ] Successfully practices "get under" at familiar cafe
- [ ] Remains calm for 2-3 minute intervals
- [ ] No barking in low-distraction situations

**Milestone 3: Consistency (Weeks 3-4)**
- [ ] Consistent calm behavior at 2-3 familiar cafes
- [ ] Can extend treat intervals to 30+ seconds
- [ ] Handles mild distractions (servers, light foot traffic)

**Milestone 4: Reliability (Weeks 4-6)**
- [ ] Reliable calm behavior across all familiar locations
- [ ] Variable interval treats working well
- [ ] Minimal to no barking incidents

### 7. Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Remi won't go under | Furniture too confining, scary | Use more open table, lure with higher value treat |
| Gets up repeatedly | Treats too infrequent, too much duration | Return to shorter intervals, more frequent rewards |
| Barking at people | Overthreshold, scared | Increase distance from triggers, redirect attention |
| Excited/panting | Too overstimulated | End session, try quieter time, exercise before |
| Won't take treats | Not hungry, too stressed | Use higher value treats, ensure adequate exercise before |

### 8. Environment Selection Criteria

**Ideal Familiar Cafe Characteristics:**
- Dog-friendly policy
- Moderate lighting (not too bright)
- Consistent schedule of visits
- Table configuration allows Remi to fully "get under"
- Not too busy during training hours
- Staff who are supportive of the training

**Cafes to Avoid Initially:**
- Very crowded/high foot traffic
- Loud music
- Other unleashed dogs
- Staff who are uncomfortable with dogs under tables
