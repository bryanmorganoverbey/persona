### Treat Interval Progression Guide

Based on reinforcement science, here is the recommended progression for extending treat intervals:

#### Phase 1: Continuous Reinforcement (Current Stage)
- **When**: Learning new behavior or in high-distraction environments
- **How**: Reward EVERY correct response
- **Interval**: Every 5-15 seconds of calm behavior
- **Goal**: Build strong association between "get under" and reward

#### Phase 2: Fixed Interval Reinforcement
- **When**: Remi reliably stays under for 5+ minutes in familiar cafes
- **How**: Reward after fixed time periods (e.g., every 30 seconds, then every 60 seconds)
- **Interval progression**: 
  - Week 1: Every 15 seconds
  - Week 2: Every 30 seconds  
  - Week 3: Every 45 seconds
  - Week 4: Every 60 seconds

#### Phase 3: Variable Interval Reinforcement (Target)
- **When**: Behavior is reliable (90%+ success in familiar cafes)
- **How**: Unpredictable timing — sometimes 20 seconds, sometimes 90 seconds
- **Why**: Prevents "strike" behavior (like a vending machine that stops working)
- **Average interval**: Every 45-90 seconds

#### Phase 4: Real-Life Rewards & Variable Ratio
- **When**: Solid reliability achieved
- **How**: Mix food treats with:
  - Verbal praise ("Good calm!")
  - Petting/attention
  - Permission to sniff (released from "under")
  - Small sips of your drink
  - Short breaks to stretch

**Important**: Never completely stop rewarding — transition to unpredictable (variable) rewards to maintain motivation.

### Best Practices for Cafe Training Success

Based on research from professional dog trainers and cafe dog owners:

#### Before the Cafe Visit
1. **Exercise first**: 20-30 minute walk before cafe time to tire out energy
2. **Partial meal**: Feed a portion of meal beforehand so dog is calm but not starving
3. **Choose timing**: Early mornings or between meal rushes are easier
4. **Pick quieter spots**: Start away from high-traffic areas, doors, and other dogs

#### At the Cafe
1. **Use familiar cafes first**: Build reliability before testing new locations
2. **Bring high-value treats**: Small, soft treats work best; save special treats for training
3. **Practice "get under" command**: Give command, reward when Remi goes under
4. **Treat at regular intervals**: Reward calm, quiet behavior every 15-30 seconds initially
5. **Stay calm yourself**: Dogs read your energy — be relaxed and confident
6. **Ignore minor behaviors**: Don't reward attention-seeking by looking at or talking to Remi when barking/whining
7. **Use a mat/bed**: Familiar mat can help generalize "this is my calm place"

#### Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Gets up frequently | Too long between treats | Shorten interval, use higher value treats |
| Barking at people | Overstimulated | Move to quieter spot, practice "settle" command |
| Won't go under table | Uncomfortable/sensory | Use familiar mat, practice at home first |
| Only works with treats visible | Too dependent on food | Fade treat visibility, use variable rewards |
| Good at home, fails at cafe | Distraction level too high | Start with shorter sessions, more treats |

### "Get Under" Command Training Checklist

Before achieving consistent cafe behavior, ensure these prerequisites are met:

- ☐ Remi reliably responds to "get under" at home (100% success)
- ☐ Remi responds to "get under" in low-distraction outdoor environments
- ☐ Remi can hold "under" position for 5+ minutes at home
- ☐ Remi can hold "under" position for 3+ minutes in quiet outdoor cafes
- ☐ Remi shows calm body language (relaxed ears, not fixated on movement)
- ☐ Remi does not bark/whine when people walk by
- ☐ Pre-cafe exercise routine is established

---

### New Environment Testing Protocol

When ready to test in unfamiliar restaurants:

1. **Scout first**: Visit without Remi to assess noise level, dog-friendliness, space under tables
2. **Start during off-peak hours**: Avoid crowded/loud times
3. **Bring everything**: Mat, high-value treats, water
4. **Set expectations lower**: Accept shorter calm periods initially
5. **Return to familiar locations**: Alternate between new and familiar to build confidence
6. **Track environment factors**: Note what made new location harder (noise, people, other dogs)

### Data Collection Framework

Key metrics to track over time:

**Primary Metrics:**
- Duration of calm behavior per session (minutes)
- Number of treats given per session
- Treat interval achieved (seconds)
- Success rate (% of session without problem behaviors)

**Secondary Metrics:**
- Pre-session exercise duration
- Time of day
- Cafe crowd level (1-5)
- Days since last cafe session
- Whether location was familiar or new

**Progress Indicators:**
- Increasing duration with same treat frequency = progress
- Decreasing treats with same duration = progress
- Successful new environment tests = progress
- Less time to settle after arrival = progress
