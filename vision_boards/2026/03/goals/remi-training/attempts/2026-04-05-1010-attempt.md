# Remi Public Behavior Training: Extending Treat Intervals

## Summary

This deliverable provides a research-based framework for extending treat intervals during Remi's "get under" training at cafes, restaurants, and events. Drawing from behavioral science on variable interval reinforcement schedules, this guide outlines a systematic approach to gradually reducing treat frequency while maintaining calm, quiet behavior in public settings.

---

## Findings / Results

### Understanding Variable Interval Reinforcement

Based on research from the American Kennel Club and canine behavior experts, the optimal approach for maintaining calm behavior with reduced treats is a **variable interval (VI) schedule**. Key findings:

- **Variable schedules produce steady, consistent response rates** without the post-reinforcement pauses seen in fixed schedules
- **Behaviors maintained on intermittent schedules are more resistant to extinction** — meaning Remi will maintain calm behavior even when treats aren't given consistently
- **Random timing keeps dogs engaged** — unlike predictable schedules where dogs "give up" between rewards, variable intervals keep Remi attentive and motivated

### The Science Behind Extending Intervals

| Schedule Type | Effect on Behavior | Best For |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Continuous (every time) | Fast learning but rapid extinction when stopped | Initial training phase |
| Fixed Interval (e.g., every 30 seconds) | Predictable, performance drops after reward | Building basic duration |
| **Variable Interval (e.g., ~30 sec average, random)** | **Steady, consistent, resistant to extinction** | **Maintaining calm behavior** |

## Phased Extension Protocol

### Phase 1: Baseline Establishment
**Current treat frequency:** Assume 1 treat every 30-60 seconds during calm behavior
**Duration:** 2-3 sessions at familiar cafes

**Actions:**
- Continue current training as baseline
- Record current treat frequency and Remi's response
- Note any triggers or moments when calm breaks

### Phase 2: Gradual Extension
**Target:** Extend average interval to 60-90 seconds using variable timing

**Implementation:**
1. **Start at 45-second average** (range: 30-60 seconds randomly)
2. Use a timer with randomized intervals OR estimate varying times between treats
3. Reward pattern example: wait 30s → treat, wait 50s → treat, wait 40s → treat, wait 70s → treat
4. **Never go more than 2 minutes without at least acknowledging** calm behavior (soft "good girl" or pet)

### Phase 3: Extended Intervals
**Target:** 90-120 second average intervals

**Implementation:**
1. Vary treats from 60s to 150s randomly
2. Mix in "life rewards" — opportunities to sniff, brief attention, or release from under the table
3. Use differential reinforcement: reward only the calmest moments, not just any quiet moment

### Phase 4: Maintenance
**Target:** Variable schedule with occasional treats only

**Implementation:**
1. Treat every 2-3 minutes on average (randomized)
2. Reserve treats for: particularly calm moments, after recovering from a distraction, or when asked to "get under" in a new location
3. Incorporate environmental rewards (sniff breaks, "all done" release)

## Practical Tips for Cafe/Restaurant Settings

1. **Use a random timer app** — Set phone to vibrate at random intervals (30s, 90s, 45s, 120s, etc.) so you don't create a predictable pattern

2. **Mix treat types** — Sometimes a small treat, sometimes just attention, sometimes nothing (variable ratio element)

3. **Watch for pre-trigger signals** — Before Remi gets antsy, that's when you reinforce calm

4. **New environments = reset** — When visiting a new cafe, temporarily reduce intervals back to baseline before extending again

5. **End on a positive** — Give a treat when you release Remi from under the table, regardless of interval timing

## Warning Signs (When to Keep Intervals Shorter)

- Pacing or shifting position frequently
- Pawing at floor or table
- Whining or轻声 vocalizing

- Breaking "get under" position
