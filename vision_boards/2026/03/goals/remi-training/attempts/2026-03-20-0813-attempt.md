## 3. Treat Interval Progression Guide

Based on AKC reinforcement schedule principles and professional trainer recommendations:

### Phase 1: Continuous Reinforcement (CR)
- **When:** Learning the behavior initially
- **Interval:** Every 30-60 seconds
- **Action:** Reward EVERY calm, quiet response
- **Goal:** Build strong association between "get under" and rewards

### Phase 2: Fixed Interval (FI) — Introduction
- **When:** Reliable "get under" response (90%+ success)
- **Interval:** Every 1-2 minutes
- **Action:** Reward after consistent calm periods
- **Note:** Predictable timing — dog learns the routine

### Phase 3: Variable Interval (VI) — Intermediate
- **When:** Solid behavior in familiar environments
- **Interval:** Randomly 1-4 minutes (average 2 min)
- **Action:** Unpredictable rewards maintain engagement
- **Why it works:** Like pop quizzes — dog stays ready

### Phase 4: Extended Variable Interval — Advanced
- **When:** Very reliable in familiar cafes
- **Interval:** Randomly 3-7 minutes (average 5 min)
- **Goal:** Maintain behavior with minimal treats

### Progression Criteria (Before Moving to Next Phase)

| Current Phase | Before Advancing |
|---------------|------------------|
| CR → FI | 90%+ success rate, 3+ sessions |
| FI → VI | Calm for full interval without prompting |
| VI → Extended | 5+ consecutive sessions without incidents |

### Important Notes on Extending Intervals

- **Never extend too fast** — If Remi breaks calm behavior, regress one phase
- **Life rewards count** — Praise, petting, sniffing breaks are rewards too
- **Variable is key** — Don't create a pattern the dog can predict
- **Quality over quantity** — One big reward > many small ones

## 6. Best Practices Summary

### What Works (Research-Backed)
1. **"Place" or "Settle" command** — Foundation for all public calm behavior
2. **Mat training** — Brings familiar comfort spot to cafes
3. **Progressive exposure** — Start easy, gradually increase difficulty
4. **Reward calm, not just obedience** — Catch moments of self-settling
5. **Short sessions initially** — 5-10 minutes, build up gradually

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Moving too fast** — Skipping from familiar to new environments too quickly
2. **Inconsistent reinforcement** — Rewarding sometimes, not others
3. **Ending on failure** — Always end sessions on a positive note
4. **Ignoring body language** — Stress signals mean it's time to leave
