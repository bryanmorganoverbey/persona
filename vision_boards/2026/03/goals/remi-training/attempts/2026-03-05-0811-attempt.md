# Remi Public Behavior Training: Consistent Calm Behavior in Familiar Cafes

## March Target: Consistent calm behavior in familiar cafes

### 1. Training Protocol

#### Pre-Session Setup
- **Identify 2-3 familiar cafes** where Remi has already had positive experiences
- **Bring**: High-value treats (cheese, boiled chicken, freeze-dried liver), training leash, optional: portable mat/bed
- **Timing**: Go during quieter hours initially (weekday mornings, early afternoon)

#### The "Get Under" Command Protocol

**Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)**
1. Enter cafe and sit at your regular table
2. Give command: "Get under" while gesturing toward the space under your chair/table
3. Reward immediately when Remi goes under and settles
4. Treat frequency: Every 30-60 seconds of calm behavior
5. Session length: 15-20 minutes

**Phase 2: Building Reliability (Weeks 2-3)**
1. Increase time between treats to every 2-3 minutes
2. Add mild distractions (staff walking by, other customers)
3. Practice the "settle" position - Remi should be lying down, not sitting alert
4. Session length: 20-30 minutes

**Phase 3: Extending Duration (Weeks 3-4)**
1. Variable interval schedule: Treat every 3-5 minutes (unpredictable)
2. Practice in slightly busier times
3. Add "release" cue: "Okay!" to end the under-table time
4. Session length: 30-45 minutes

#### Key Training Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Continuous Reinforcement** | Reward every time Remi responds correctly initially |
| **Variable Ratio Schedule** | After learning, reward unpredictably (every 2-8 minutes) - this builds strongest reliability |
| **Differential Reinforcement** | Only reward the calmest, quietest positions; ignore fidgeting |
| **Life Rewards** | Mix treats with opportunities to sniff, greet friendly staff, or exit |

### 3. Success Criteria for "Consistent Calm Behavior"

**Target Benchmarks:**
- [ ] Remi goes under table within 5 seconds of command
- [ ] Remi remains settled for minimum 10 consecutive minutes without treats
- [ ] Zero barking incidents in 3+ consecutive sessions at same location
- [ ] Remi settles voluntarily (without command) after being released
- [ ] Successfully handles mild distractions (servers, other dogs passing by)

**Behavioral Indicators of Success:**
- Relaxed body posture (lying down, not sitting alert)
- Deep sighs, relaxed ears
- Eyes soft or partially closed
- Responds to name when called from under table
- Does not strain at leash or show stress signals

### 4. Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Remi won't settle | Start with more frequent treats, then gradually extend; ensure adequate exercise before session |
| Barking at other dogs/people | Create more distance from trigger; use higher value treats; practice "look at me" redirect |
| Getting up repeatedly | Don't reward until settled; use release cue properly; reduce session length |
| Only works for treats | Mix in life rewards (sniff break, praise, access to outdoor); use variable schedule |
| Stress signals (pacing, lip licking, whale eye) | End session early; return to quieter environment; reduce duration |
