### 🐕 Remi Public Behavior Training: New Environment Testing Plan

**Goal Context:** Remi is being trained to sit quietly under chairs/tables at cafes, restaurants, and events without barking or making noise. The "get under" command is the primary cue, with treat reinforcement at regular intervals to reward calm behavior.

---

### Phase 1: Preparation (Before Visiting New Restaurant)

Before taking Remi to an unfamiliar venue, prepare with these steps:

1. **Pre-visit reconnaissance** — If possible, visit the restaurant first without Remi to assess:
   - Patio vs. indoor options
   - Noise levels (music, TV, kitchen sounds)
   - Foot traffic and crowding patterns
   - Whether other dogs are typically present

2. **Pack the training kit:**
   - High-value treats (more appealing than usual — consider chicken, cheese, or freeze-dried liver)
   - Portable water bowl
   - Familiar blanket or mat (helps create "safe space" association)
   - Leash and collar/harness
   - Clicker (if using clicker training)
   - Small notebook or phone for data tracking

3. **Timing matters** — Choose off-peak hours for initial visits:
   - Weekdays before 12pm or after 2pm
   - Avoid weekend peak hours, happy hour, or Friday/Saturday nights

---

### Phase 2: First Visit to New Environment

**Principle from research:** Keep the dog below threshold — the level at which Remi doesn't respond negatively. Start easy, build success.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Arrive and request a quiet corner table, preferably against a wall |
| 2 | Ask for "get under" immediately — Remi goes under chair/table |
| 3 | Give first treat within 30 seconds of settling |
| 4 | Treat at 1-minute intervals for first 5-10 minutes |
| 5 | Observe body language — if relaxed, extend to 2-minute intervals |
| 6 | If Remi shows stress (whining, panting, pacing, stiff posture), end session early |

**Success criteria for first visit:**
- Remi settles within 2 minutes
- No barking or whining
- Can remain calm for at least 10 minutes
- Shows relaxed body language (loose posture, possibly lying down)

---

### Phase 3: Progressive New Environment Testing

Following the desensitization approach, gradually increase difficulty:

| Level | Environment Type | Treat Interval | Duration Goal |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Level 1** | Familiar cafe (already trained) | 3-5 minutes | 45+ minutes |
| **Level 2** | New cafe, low traffic | 2-3 minutes | 30 minutes |
| **Level 3** | New cafe/restaurant, moderate traffic | 1-2 minutes | 25 minutes |
| **Level 4** | Unfamiliar restaurant, busy patio | 1 minute | 20 minutes |
| **Level 5** | High-stimulation environment | 30-60 seconds | 15-20 minutes |

**Move to next level only when:**
- Current level achieved with 90%+ success rate
- Remi shows relaxed body language consistently
- No barking or noise incidents in 3+ sessions

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| **Reluctance to "get under" in new space** | Practice the command at home in different rooms first; use familiar blanket as anchor |
| **Barking at servers/other patrons** | Pre-empt with treat before people approach; use higher value treats |
| **Distracted by other dogs** | Increase distance from other dogs; treat before Remi notices them |
| **Pacing or inability to settle** | End session early — it's better to leave on a success than push through stress |
| **Refusal to take treats** | Environment is too stimulating; reduce intensity or leave |

---

### Key Principles from Research

1. **Desensitization and counterconditioning** — Pair new environments with positive experiences (treats, praise). Keep Remi below threshold — if stressed, back off.

2. **Gradual progression** — Don't rush. Each new environment is a training opportunity. Build from familiar → similar → different.

3. **Watch body language** — Relaxed posture, soft eyes, willingness to take treats = success. Stiff posture, whale eye, panting, whining = stress signal.

4. **Always have an exit plan** — If the session isn't working, leave calmly. Preservation of positive associations is more important than duration.
