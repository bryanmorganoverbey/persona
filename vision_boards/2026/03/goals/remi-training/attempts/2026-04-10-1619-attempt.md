## Quick Reference: Calm Behavior Criteria

Based on professional service dog training standards:

| Level | Duration Under Command | Treat Interval | Environment |
|-------|------------------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Beginner** | 1-3 minutes | Every 30-60 seconds | Quiet, familiar |
| **Developing** | 3-5 minutes | Every 60-90 seconds | Moderate distraction |
| **Proficient** | 5-10 minutes | Every 90-120 seconds | Varying distractions |
| **Advanced** | 10-20 minutes | Every 2-3 minutes | Novel environments |

**Calm Behavior Indicators** (from Humane Society of Missouri):
- Sitting with one leg tucked under body
- Relaxed facial muscles
- Breathing regularly and slowly
- No barking, whining, or whining
- Eyes soft/focused on handler
- Body low and settled

## Tips for Accurate Tracking

1. **Use a timer** — Start a stopwatch when you give the "get under" command
2. **Mark intervals** — Note exact time when each treat is given
3. **Be honest about issues** — Document problems to identify patterns
4. **Rate consistently** — Use the same criteria each session
5. **Review weekly** — Look for trends every 7 sessions
