## Part 3: Treat Interval Progression Guide

Based on research from the American Kennel Club on reinforcement schedules, here's the recommended progression for extending treat intervals while maintaining calm behavior.

### Phase 1: Continuous Reinforcement (CR)
**Goal:** Teach the behavior and build association
- **Treat Interval:** Every 5-10 seconds
- **When:** New environments or early learning
- **Method:** Reward every correct response immediately

### Phase 2: Fixed Duration Schedule
**Goal:** Build duration while keeping predictable rewards
- **Treat Interval:** Every 15-20 seconds
- **When:** Behavior is reliable with continuous reinforcement
- **Method:** Timer-based rewards at consistent intervals

### Phase 3: Variable Duration Schedule (Recommended)
**Goal:** Maintain behavior without constant treats
- **Average Treat Interval:** 30-45 seconds (varying between 20-60 sec)
- **When:** Solid reliability in familiar cafes
- **Method:** Random intervals prevent prediction, maintain engagement
- **Why it works:** Dogs can't predict when the reward comes, so they maintain steady behavior

### Phase 4: Intermittent Reinforcement (Maintenance)
**Goal:** Long-term reliability with minimal treats
- **Treat Interval:** Every 60-90 seconds (variable)
- **When:** Highly reliable behavior in multiple familiar locations
- **Method:** Mix of verbal praise, petting, and occasional treats
- **Note:** This schedule produces the most resilient behaviors

### Extension Protocol

| Current Interval | Target Interval | Days at Current | Success Criteria |
|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|------------------|
| 5-10 sec | 15 sec | 2-3 sessions | < 2 incidents per session |
| 15 sec | 30 sec | 3-5 sessions | < 2 incidents per session |
| 30 sec | 45 sec | 5-7 sessions | Zero incidents for 2 consecutive sessions |
| 45 sec | 60 sec | 7-10 sessions | Zero incidents for 3 consecutive sessions |

## Part 4: Consistency Metrics

### Definition of "Consistent Calm Behavior"

For a session to count as successful, Remi must:
- [ ] Respond to "get under" command within 5 seconds
- [ ] Remain under chair/table for at least 15 minutes
- [ ] Zero barking incidents
- [ ] Zero whining/whimpering incidents
- [ ] No more than 3 brief restlessness episodes (under 10 sec each)
- [ ] No pawing at handler or furniture

## Part 6: Troubleshooting Guide

### Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Won't "get under" | Distraction level too high | Start in quieter corner, use higher value treats |
| Barking at other dogs | Overthreshold | Increase distance from trigger, practiceEngagement-Disengagement game |
| Restlessness | Needs more exercise before session | Add pre-cafe walk/play session |
| Only works with visible treats | Treat dependency | Switch to random schedule, use treat pouch not visible |
| Different behavior at different cafes | Environment associations | Practice at each cafe separately, build familiarity |
