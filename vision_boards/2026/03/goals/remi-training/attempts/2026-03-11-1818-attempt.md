## 1. Training Command Reference

**Primary Command: "Get Under"**
- Target behavior: Remi goes under the chair/table and settles quietly
- This mimics the "place" command but is specifically for under-table settling
- Works well in cafes where space under tables/chairs is available

---

## 2. Reinforcement Schedule Protocol

Based on behavioral science, here's the progression for Remi:

### Phase 1: Continuous Reinforcement (Learning)
- **Treat every time** Remi successfully gets under and settles
- Build strong association between "get under" and positive outcome
- Duration: Until behavior is ~90% reliable in familiar environments

### Phase 2: Variable Interval Reinforcement (Extending)
- **Randomize treat timing** — don't treat at fixed intervals
- Use a **variable interval schedule**: treats come at unpredictable intervals
- Start with 30-60 second intervals, then gradually extend
- This prevents Remi from only being calm right before treat time

### Phase 3: Maintenance
- Occasional treats combined with **life rewards** (praise, petting, release to sniff)
- Remi should maintain behavior even without constant treats

---

## 3. Familiar Cafe Progression Guide

### Level 1: Known High-Value Location
- Visit the **same familiar cafe** consistently
- Start with shorter sessions (15-20 minutes)
- Treat frequency: every 30-60 seconds initially
- Goal: Complete silence/barking-free session

### Level 2: Extended Duration
- Gradually increase session length to 45-60 minutes
- Begin **variable interval treats** (sometimes 30 sec, sometimes 90 sec)
- Watch for: relaxed body language, lying down, sleeping

### Level 3: Reduced Treat Frequency
- Extend intervals to 2-3 minutes between treats
- Use **differential reinforcement**: only reward the calmest moments
- Phase in longer stays without treats

## 7. Training Tips & Best Practices

### Pre-Cafe Preparation:
1. **Exercise first** — a tired dog is a calm dog
2. **High-value treats** — use special treats only for training (e.g., cheese, chicken, freeze-dried liver)
3. **Avoid feeding right before** — some dogs get energetic after eating

### During Session:
1. **Bring own water** — hydration without needing to ask staff
2. **Choose corner position** — reduces foot traffic distractions
3. **Reset if over-stimulated** — go outside if Remi can't calm down
4. **Keep leash on** — safety and control

### Troubleshooting:

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Won't settle | Start with shorter sessions, more frequent treats |
| Barking at people | Move to quieter area, increase distance from traffic |
| Only calm when treats visible | Use treat pouch but don't show treats constantly |
| Gets up frequently | Practice "place" command at home first |
| Excitable arrival | Wait outside until calm, then enter |
