## 4. Treat Interval Extension Protocol

### Progression Schedule

```
Week 1-2:    30 seconds → 45 seconds (baseline)
Week 3-4:    45 seconds → 90 seconds
Week 5-6:    90 seconds → 2 minutes
Week 7+:     2 minutes → 3+ minutes (maintenance)
```

### Extension Guidelines

1. **Criteria for increasing interval:**
   - 3+ consecutive sessions without barking/loud behavior
   - Remi settles within 30 seconds of "get under" command
   - Shows relaxed body language (lying down, eyes soft)
   - Minimal scanning/alert behavior

2. **If behavior regresses:**
   - Return to previous interval for 2-3 sessions
   - Increase engagement/attention before treating
   - Reduce duration if needed

3. **Variable reinforcement:**
   - Once at 2+ minute intervals, use variable timing
   - Randomly treat at 1.5 - 3 minute intervals
   - Prevents prediction while maintaining calm

## 5. Familiar Cafe Selection Criteria

### Ideal Characteristics

| Factor | Preferred | Avoid |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Seating** | Tables with solid sides/drapes | Open layouts with high foot traffic |
| **Space** | Corner tables, against walls | Center of room |
| **Noise level** | Background music, quiet | Live music, TV |
| **Dog traffic** | Occasionally other dogs | Dog-heavy environment |
| **Staff** | Pet-aware, non-intrusive | Staff that approach/pets frequently |
| **Lighting** | Natural light, not harsh | Strobe or flashing lights |

### Recommended Cafe Profile (Mexico/Tennessee)

- Small, locally-owned coffee shops
- Outdoor patios with moderate shade
- Table spacing allows dog to be unobtrusive
- Staff experienced with dog visitors

## 6. Command & Reinforcement Reference

### Primary Commands

| Command | Cue | Method |
|---------|-----|--------|
| **Get Under** | Verbal + hand signal | Lead under table, treat immediately |
| **Settle** | Soft voice, gentle touch | Pat leg, calm tone |
| **Quiet** | Firm but calm | Hold treat, mark silence |
| **Stay** | Hand out, hold | Gradual duration increase |

### Reward Hierarchy

1. **High value:** Small meat treats, cheese
2. **Medium value:** Commercial dog treats
3. **Low value:** Kibble (for maintenance)

### Body Language Indicators

**Calm/Ready for interval extension:**
- Lying down, weight on hip
- Soft eyes, slow blinks
- Tail relaxed, neutral position
- Ears in neutral position
- Breathing steady, relaxed

**Aroused/Needs more support:**
- Alert sitting, weight forward
- Wide eyes, ears forward
- Tail up or stiff
- Panting, restless movement
- Scanning environment
