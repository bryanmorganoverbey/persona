# Remi Public Behavior Training
## March Target: Consistent Calm Behavior in Familiar Cafes

### Training Protocol

#### Pre-Session Preparation

**Timing**
- Schedule cafe visits after Remi has had substantial exercise (walk, hike, or play session)
- Aim for times when Remi is naturally more settled (typically post-exercise drowsiness)
- Avoid peak energy times (after long periods of rest)

**What to Bring**
- High-value treats (cheese, chicken, freeze-dried liver)
- Regular training treats
- Long-lasting chew (frozen Kong or bully stick) for occupation
- Water bowl
- Familiar blanket or mat (optional, for "place" association)
- Treat pouch for easy access

**Pre-Visit Warm-Up (5 minutes before leaving)**
1. Practice "get under" command at home on various surfaces
2. Work on "stay" command with increasing duration
3. Practice "leave it" with distractions
4. This reinforces expectations and mentally tires Remi

#### Cafe Session Protocol

**Arrival (0-2 minutes)**
1. Choose seating with minimal foot traffic
2. Position Remi facing away from entrance (reduces anxiety from people coming/going)
3. Allow brief settling period before commanding

**Initial Command Sequence (2-5 minutes)**
1. Attach leash (loose, not tension)
2. Give "get under" command
3. Remi goes under chair/table
4. Immediately reward with high-value treat
5. Wait 10-15 seconds, then reward again
6. Continue alternating (reward-calm-reward-calm)

**Building Duration (5-15 minutes)**
1. Gradually extend time between treats
2. Use the following progression:

| Phase | Treat Interval | Criteria |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Every 10-15 seconds | Initial learning, new environment |
| 2 | Every 30 seconds | Reliable response |
| 3 | Every 60 seconds | Consistent calm |
| 4 | Every 90 seconds-2 minutes | Established behavior |
| 5 | Variable (random intervals) | Maintenance mode |

**Maintenance Behaviors to Reward**
- Looking at you (eye contact)
- Lying down calmly
- Rolling onto side (relaxation)
- Sleeping/dozing
- Any requested behavior ("sit," "down," "stay")

**Warning Signs to Watch**
- Pacing or standing up repeatedly
- Whining or vocalizing
- Barking at people/dogs
- Lunging or excessive alertness
- Lip licking or yawning (stress signals)

**If Warning Signs Appear**
1. Decrease distance from distraction
2. Return to shorter treat intervals
3. Use a long-lasting chew for occupation
4. Consider ending session early if stress persists
5. Don't punish — redirect and reward calm

#### Session End
1. Give release word ("okay," "free")
2. Allow brief stretch break
3. Reward calm behavior during transition
4. Praise heavily when leaving calmly

### Treat Interval Extension Guidelines

#### When to Advance to Next Interval

**Ready for Extension When:**
- [ ] Remi reliably stays under table for full interval without stress signals
- [ ] Calm behavior (lying down, relaxing) observed during most of interval
- [ ] Commands ("get under," "stay") responded to promptly 90%+ of time
- [ ] No barking or vocalizing during session
- [ ] Recovery quickly if disturbed (door, passerby)

**Hold at Current Level When:**
- [ ] Stress signals appear (pacing, whining, lip licking)
- [ ] Command response drops below 80%
- [ ] Multiple sessions with regression
- [ ] New environment or increased distractions

#### Variable Reinforcement Schedule (Maintenance Phase)

Once Remi is reliable at 2-minute intervals:

1. Randomize treat timing (like a slot machine)
2. Reward 40-60% of intervals
3. Always reward: exceptionally good responses, new environments, high distraction
4. Sometimes reward: normal calm behavior
5. Occasionally withhold: to test reliability

**Key Principle:** Remi should never know exactly when the treat is coming, but should always know it's possible.

### Tips from Professional Trainers

1. **Set up for success** — Go to cafe after exercise when Remi is naturally tired
2. **Manage environment** — Choose low-traffic seating, face dog away from entrance
3. **Stay proactive** — Monitor for distractions before Remi reacts
4. **Keep initial visits short** — Start with coffee/snack only, build to full meal
5. **Be flexible** — If Remi is having an off day, leave early and try again
6. **Use real-life rewards** — Sometimes praise, petting, or access to sniff is enough
7. **Practice at home first** — Warm up skills before going to cafe

---

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Won't go under table | Command not generalized | Practice on various tables at home |
| Gets up repeatedly | Too long between treats | Shorten interval, increase treat value |
| Barks at people | Overstimulated/stressed | Increase distance, use chew for occupation |
| Won't take treats | Not hungry or too stressed | Train before feeding, use higher value treats |
| Good at home, not cafe | Distraction level too high | Start with quieter venues, build gradually |
