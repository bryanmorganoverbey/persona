# Remi Public Behavior Training: Extending Treat Intervals

## Objective
Gradually reduce treat frequency while maintaining Remi's calm, quiet behavior under chairs/tables at cafes, restaurants, and events.

## Understanding Reinforcement Schedules

### Current Stage: Continuous Reinforcement (CR)
Currently, Remi receives a treat for every instance of calm behavior under the table. This is appropriate for the learning phase but needs to transition to maintain reliability while reducing treat volume.

### Target: Variable Interval (VI) Schedule
Based on AKC research, a variable interval schedule provides rewards after unpredictable time intervals. This creates steady, reliable behavior without requiring constant treats.

---

## Gradual Extension Protocol

### Phase 1: Initial Extension (Current → 2-3 min intervals)

**Target Interval:** 2 minutes between treats
**Method:**
1. After Remi settles under the chair, wait 2 minutes before delivering the first treat
2. Use verbal praise ("good settle") during the wait to acknowledge calm behavior
3. Continue with treats every 2 minutes initially
4. Vary the interval slightly (90 seconds to 2.5 minutes) to prevent predictable patterns

**Success Criteria:** Remi remains calm, settled, and quiet for full 2-minute intervals

### Phase 2: Moderate Extension (3-5 min intervals)

**Target Interval:** 3-5 minutes between treats
**Method:**
1. Once Phase 1 is reliable (3+ sessions without regression), extend to 3 minutes
2. Add environmental rewards: brief attention from you, a gentle stroke under the chin
3. Still provide actual treats but less frequently
4. Use unpredictable timing: sometimes 3 min, sometimes 4.5 min, sometimes 5 min

**Success Criteria:** 3 consecutive successful sessions at 3-minute minimum intervals

### Phase 3: Advanced Extension (5-10 min intervals)

**Target Interval:** 5-10 minutes between treats
**Method:**
1. Mix in "life rewards": release to sniff during walk to cafe, extended petting session
2. Use variable intervals: 5, 7, 8, 10 minutes (unpredictable)
3. Deliver treats at high-value moments (when other customers arrive, during peak noise)
4. Sometimes skip a treat interval entirely and reward with praise only

**Success Criteria:** Remi maintains calm behavior for 10+ minute stretches

### Phase 4: Maintenance (Variable schedule)

**Target:** Intermittent reinforcement
**Method:**
- Treats become unpredictable rewards (like a "slot machine")
- Mix of: actual treats, verbal praise, physical affection, life rewards (sniff breaks, access to special spots)
- 70% of the time: verbal praise or life rewards only
- 30% of the time: actual treats (random intervals)
- Results in highly resilient behavior resistant to extinction

## Key Principles

1. **One Variable at a Time:** Don't extend intervals when introducing new environments. Use familiar cafes for interval extension work.

2. **Regression is Normal:** If Remi backslides, return to previous interval for 1-2 sessions before attempting extension again.

3. **Watch for Stress Signals:** Extended intervals can cause anxiety. Look for:
   - Pacing or shifting positions
   - Lip licking
   - Whining or soft vocalizations
   - Looking at you persistently

4. **High-Value Moments:** Still reward during challenging moments (loud noises, other dogs passing, server visits) regardless of interval schedule.

5. **Environment Matters:** Start interval extension in calm environments before testing in busier venues.
