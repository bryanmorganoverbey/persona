## Key Metrics to Track

### Primary Metrics
1. **Duration of calm behavior** — Total minutes Remi remained calm and settled under furniture
2. **Success rate** — Percentage of session time spent calm vs. total session time
3. **Treat interval extension** — How many minutes between treats (goal: gradually increase)

### Secondary Metrics
1. **Incident count** — Number of barking/whining episodes per session
2. **Recovery time** — How quickly Remi settles after a disturbance
3. **Location progression** — Familiar → New environments

---

## Progressive Treat Schedule Guide

### Phase 1: Initial Training (Familiar Locations)
- Treat every 1-2 minutes
- Goal: Establish calm behavior baseline

### Phase 2: Familiar Locations — Extend Intervals
- Week 1: Treat every 2 minutes
- Week 2: Treat every 3 minutes
- Week 3: Treat every 5 minutes
- Week 4: Treat every 7-10 minutes

### Phase 3: New Locations
- Start at treat every 2 minutes (reset)
- Work up to 5-minute intervals before extending

### Phase 4: Maintenance
- Treat every 10-15 minutes or random rewards
- Focus on proofing in varied environments

# Remi Calm Behavior — Analysis Guide

## How to Use This Data

### Weekly Analysis
At the end of each week, calculate:
- **Average calm duration** — Sum of calm minutes ÷ number of sessions
- **Success rate** — (Calm duration ÷ Total duration) × 100
- **Treat efficiency** — Total treats ÷ Calm duration (lower = more efficient)
- **Incident trend** — Are incidents decreasing?

### Monthly Analysis
- Compare average calm duration month-over-month
- Track progress in new vs. familiar locations
- Note which environments cause most incidents
- Measure treat interval extension progress

### Progress Indicators (Green Flags)
✅ Calm duration increasing
✅ Treat intervals extending (fewer treats per minute)
✅ Incidents decreasing
✅ Success ratings improving
✅ New locations becoming easier

### Warning Signs (Red Flags)
⚠️ Calm duration decreasing
⚠️ Incidents increasing
⚠️ Regressing in familiar locations
⚠️ Unable to extend treat intervals
