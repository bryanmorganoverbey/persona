## Section 1: Extending Treat Intervals

### The Science: Variable Interval Reinforcement

Based on training research, Remi is currently on a **continuous reinforcement schedule** (rewarded every 30-60 seconds). To extend her duration while maintaining behavior, we should transition to a **variable interval (VI) schedule**.

**Why Variable Over Fixed?**
- Fixed interval: Predictable timing → dog relaxes right after treats, gets restless near reward time
- Variable interval: Unpredictable timing → dog maintains steady, calm behavior throughout

### Progressive Interval Extension Plan

**Week 1-2 (March 1-14):**
- Current: 30-60 second intervals
- Transition to: 60-90 second intervals
- Method: Randomize timing (not every 60 seconds exactly)

**Week 3-4 (March 15-31):**
- Target: 90-120 second intervals
- Method: Mix of 60, 90, 120 second intervals (average ~90)

**Week 5-6 (April, if continued):**
- Target: 2-3 minute intervals
- Method: Random intervals between 2-3 minutes

### Sample Variable Interval Schedule

| Session | Target Average | Example Timing (seconds) |
|---------|----------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | 60s | 45, 70, 55, 80, 60 |
| 2 | 75s | 60, 90, 65, 85, 75 |
| 3 | 90s | 75, 100, 80, 110, 90 |

**Key Principle:** Always reward *after* the interval passes AND Remi is still calm. Never punish getting up—just prompt "get under" and reset.

## Section 2: Managing the Trigger (Other Dogs)

### Understanding Remi's Trigger

**Trigger Profile:**
- Primary: Small dogs
- Context: Stray dogs entering cafes
- Reaction: barking, getting agitated

### Threshold Management Strategy

**What is Threshold?**
The distance/level of stimulus at which Remi can still:
- Notice the trigger
- Remain calm enough to take treats
- Respond to cues

**Current Assumption:** Remi's threshold with small dogs is likely quite close (within 10-15 feet). Stray dogs walking directly into the cafe may be *above* threshold.

### Practical Management in Cafes

**1. Pre-Emptive Positioning**
- Choose table locations with back to wall (fewer approach angles)
- Position to see entrance—Remi can see but isn't surprised
- Sit with Remi between you and the wall/away from foot traffic

**2. Early Warning System**
- Watch for dogs entering BEFORE Remi notices
- If you see a dog coming, have treats ready
- Start treat delivery BEFORE Remi reacts

**3. The "Look at That" Game (Leslie McDevitt Method)**

When a dog appears:
1. Wait for Remi to notice the dog
2. Say "yes" (or click) the MOMENT she looks at the dog
3. Immediately follow with a treat
4. Repeat—dog appears → Remi looks → "yes" → treat

**Goal:** Remi learns: *Dog appearing = treats coming from handler* rather than *Dog appearing = alert/bark*

**4. Emergency Protocol**
If a dog is too close and Remi is going over threshold:
- Have a high-value treat (hot dog) ready
- Use "get under" command with treats delivered under the table
- Create distance if possible
- Speak calmly—the calmer you are, the calmer Remi will be

### Desensitization & Counterconditioning (Long-Term)

**The Process:**
1. **Find threshold distance:** How close can a dog be before Remi reacts?
2. **Start below threshold:** Dog at distance where Remi notices but stays calm
3. **Pair with treats:** Every time Remi sees a dog → treats appear
4. **Gradually decrease distance:** Very slowly over many sessions
5. **Add variables:** Still dog → moving dog → dog with sounds

**Important:** This takes time. Multiple short sessions (5-10 minutes) are better than long ones. Progress may be measured in weeks/months.

## Section 4: Recommended Training Sessions Per Week

### Minimum Viable Practice
- 3 sessions per week in familiar cafes
- 15-20 minutes per session

### Optimal Practice
- 5-6 sessions per week
- Mix of familiar locations and 1-2 new environments
- 20-30 minutes per session

### Session Structure

```
PRE-SESSION (2 min):
- Warm-up: Practice "get under" command at home or outside
- Treats ready: Cut hot dogs into small pieces

DURING SESSION:
- 0-5 min: Settling in, frequent treats to establish calm
- 5-10 min: Extend first interval, begin variable timing
- 10-20 min: Main duration work, stay under threshold
- Last 5 min: Challenge slightly (if doing well)

END SESSION:
- Give release command
- Positive verbal praise
- Store remaining treats for next session

TRIGGER MANAGEMENT:
- If dog appears: Start "Look at That" immediately
- Stay below threshold
- Have highest value treats (hot dogs) for trigger events
```

## Section 6: Troubleshooting

### If Remi Gets Restless Before Interval Ends

**Signs:** Pawing, shifting position, looking up
**Solution:**
- Ignore the behavior (don't reward getting up)
- Say "settle" or "under" in calm voice
- Wait for her to lie back down
- Treat when she's calm again

### If She Can't Hold 60 Seconds

**Solution:** Revert to 30 seconds for 2-3 sessions, then try again. Every dog progresses at different speeds.

### If Trigger Events Cause Regression

**Solution:** This is normal. Go back to shorter intervals for 1-2 sessions. Return to baseline and rebuild.
