## 1. Training Philosophy & Core Principles

Based on research from AKC, GoPetFriendly, and professional dog trainers:

### Key Success Factors
- **Set up for success**: Begin training at home, then progress to low-distraction environments before advancing to busy cafes
- **Start with a relaxed dog**: Exercise Remi before cafe visits (e.g., 1-mile walk) to reduce excess energy
- **Keep visits short initially**: 5-10 minutes for first sessions, gradually extending duration
- **Choose ideal seating**: Corner spots away from foot traffic and other dogs
- **Leave when Remi stops having fun**: End sessions on a positive note before fatigue sets in

---

## 2. The "Get Under" Command Protocol

### Step-by-Step Training Method

| Phase | Description | Treat Timing |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Phase 1: Home Practice** | Practice "get under" command under household table with Remi's mat/towel | Treat every 5-10 seconds of calm behavior |
| **Phase 2: Low-Stimulation Outdoors** | Practice at quiet outdoor seating areas (park picnics) | Treat every 15-30 seconds |
| **Phase 3: Familiar Cafes (Slow Times)** | Visit cafes during quiet hours (early morning, mid-afternoon) | Treat every 30-60 seconds |
| **Phase 4: Familiar Cafes (Normal Hours)** | Build duration during regular business hours | Treat every 60-90 seconds |
| **Phase 5: New Environments** | Test in unfamiliar pet-friendly restaurants | Adjust based on comfort level |

### Command Cues
- **Primary**: "Get under" — Remi goes under chair/table
- **Release**: "Okay!" or "Free!" — Signals end of settle
- **Reinforcement marker**: "Yes!" or clicker — Marks desired behavior

## 3. Treat Interval Progression System

### Gradual Extension Guidelines

Based on variable-interval reinforcement research:

| Training Stage | Interval Between Treats | Session Duration Goal |
|----------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **Week 1-2** | Every 5 seconds | 5-10 minutes |
| **Week 3-4** | Every 15 seconds | 10-15 minutes |
| **Week 5-6** | Every 30 seconds | 15-20 minutes |
| **Week 7-8** | Every 60 seconds | 20-30 minutes |
| **Week 9+** | Variable (30-90 sec) | 30+ minutes |

### Key Principles
- **Only extend intervals when behavior is RELIABLE** — If Remi breaks settle, return to shorter intervals
- **Variable reinforcement** — Once reliable, mix up treat timing to strengthen the behavior
- **Always have high-value treats ready** — For challenging distractions

## 6. Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Problem: Remi Won't Settle
- **Cause**: Overstimulated, too hungry/tired, or not enough exercise
- **Solution**: Start with longer pre-cafe walk; choose quieter times; bring stuffed toy/Kong

### Problem: Barking at Other Dogs/People
- **Solution**: Move to more isolated seating; use "leave it" command; have high-value treats ready
- **If persists**: Leave immediately and reset in car/outside

### Problem: Focuses on Food/Steals Food
- **Solution**: Scan floor before sitting; keep Remi on leash; use "leave it" command
- **Prevention**: Never feed from table; Remi should never receive human food at cafe

### Problem: Whines When You Eat
- **Solution**: Ignore completely; only treat when quiet; practice "hush" command at home first

### Problem: Anxiety When You Leave Table
- **Solution**: Practice scenarios where one person steps away; reward calm during absence

## 7. Cafe Selection Criteria for Training

### Ideal Training Cafes (Familiar Environment)
- [ ] Pet-friendly policy confirmed
- [ ] Outdoor seating available
- [ ] Moderate foot traffic (not empty, not packed)
- [ ] Adequate shade coverage
- [ ] Tables with sufficient under-space
- [ ] Water bowls provided/allowed
- [ ] Staff experienced with dog patrons
- [ ] Located in familiar neighborhood

### Progression: Familiar → New Environments
1. **Master** 3-5 familiar cafes first
2. **Visit** new cafe during quiet hours
3. **Progress** to normal busy hours
4. **Test** different types (coffee shop → restaurant → brewery)
