## Overview

This document provides the training protocol and tracking system for achieving consistent calm behavior in familiar cafe environments. The protocol builds on the foundational "get under" command and introduces graduated challenges specific to cafe settings.

**Assumed Baseline:** Remi already understands the "get under" command and has demonstrated the ability to settle calmly in low-distraction environments.

---

## Step-by-Step Training Protocol

### Phase 1: Establishing Reliability in Familiar Cafes

Before progressing to new environments, Remi should demonstrate consistent calm behavior in 3-5 familiar cafe locations. A "familiar" cafe is one visited at least 3 times with positive training sessions.

**Session Structure:**

1. **Pre-Session (10-15 minutes before entering):**
   - Exercise Remi sufficiently (walk or play) to reduce baseline excitement
   - Complete bathroom break
   - Conduct a brief "get under" practice in a low-distraction area

2. **Entry Protocol:**
   - Enter during off-peak hours initially (aim for <50% capacity)
   - Request a table with adequate under-space and minimal foot traffic
   - Position Remi on the side away from walkways
   - Offer water immediately to settle

3. **Initial Settling (first 5 minutes):**
   - Give "get under" command
   - Deliver treats at 30-second intervals for calm positioning
   - Ignore any attention-seeking behavior (whining, pawing, etc.)
   - Only reward calm, quiet behavior

4. **Extended Stay (after initial settling):**
   - Gradually extend treat intervals to 1 minute, then 2 minutes
   - Remi must maintain calm for full interval before receiving treat
   - If Remi becomes restless, return to shorter intervals

### Phase 2: Cafe Selection Criteria

**Ideal Cafe Characteristics:**

- Outdoor or partially outdoor seating (easier exit if needed)
- Tables with solid aprons (no cross-supports blocking under-space)
- Moderate but not heavy foot traffic
- Staff comfortable with dogs
- Water available upon request
- Ability to sit for 30-60 minutes

**Cafes to Avoid Initially:**

- High foot traffic areas (near door, pathway to restrooms)
- Tables with complex under-structures
- Very crowded environments
- Locations where Remi can see other dogs or reactive triggers

### Phase 3: Managing Distractions

In familiar cafes, common distractions include:

- Wait staff approaching
- Other patrons passing close
- Food being delivered nearby
- External stimuli (people walking by window, sounds)

**Response Protocol:**

| Distraction Type | Response |
|------------------|----------|
| Staff approaching | Quietly praise Remi for staying calm, deliver treat before staff arrives |
| Patron passing | Remi should not be rewarded for looking; wait for head to lower, then treat |
| Food delivery | Redirect Remi's attention with a chew or puzzle toy; treat for ignoring food |
| External noise | Mark calm behavior with "yes" or click, then treat |

## Extending Treat Intervals: The Protocol

Treat interval extension should follow this progression:

**Week 1-2:** 30-second intervals (high rate)
**Week 3-4:** 45-60 second intervals
**Week 5-6:** 60-90 second intervals
**Week 7+:** 90-120 second intervals

**Rules for Extension:**

1. Only extend when Remi is consistently calm throughout the interval
2. If Remi breaks calm (stands up, whines, paws), reset to shorter interval
3. Do not extend more than one "level" per week
4. Always maintain one successful "reset" before attempting extension

**Proofing Behaviors:**

When treat intervals are extended, the following behaviors should remain solid:

- Remi remains under the table without being told
- No barking or whining
- No attention-seeking behavior
- Remi settles into a rest position (lying down, chin on paws)
- Remi does not react to mild distractions

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue: Remi becomes restless after 15-20 minutes

**Possible causes:**

- Needs more exercise before session
- Over-stimulated by environment
- Tired but too anxious to settle

**Solutions:**

- Increase pre-session exercise
- Reduce session duration until stamina builds
- Use a calming supplement (consult vet first)
- Bring a familiar mat or blanket for comfort

### Issue: Remi barks at other dogs or patrons

**Solutions:**

- Request a more isolated table
- Create more distance from triggers
- Return to higher treat rate to reinforce quiet
- If barking persists, end session and try again another day

### Issue: Remi will not settle under table

**Solutions:**

- Practice "get under" at home first
- Start with shorter sessions
- Use a mat to define the space
- Try different table positions

### Issue: Remi is "on alert" constantly

**Solutions:**

- Accept this is normal for some dogs in public
- Reward any brief moments of relaxation
- Do not over-correct; this increases arousal
- Be patient—alertness decreases with experience

## Safety & Exit Protocol

**Always be prepared to leave if:**

- Remi becomes reactive and cannot be redirected
- Barking disturbs other patrons
- Remi is overly stressed (panting, drooling, trembling)
- Environment becomes too crowded or chaotic

**Exit process:**

1. Remain calm—do not make leaving a negative experience
2. Give Remi a command to come out from under table
3. Gather belongings efficiently
4. Exit calmly without scolding Remi
5. Once outside, allow Remi to decompress before continuing
