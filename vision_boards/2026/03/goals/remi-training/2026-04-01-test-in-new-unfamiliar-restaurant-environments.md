# Remi Public Behavior Training: New Environment Testing Plan

## Summary

This deliverable provides a comprehensive framework for testing Remi's public behavior training in new and unfamiliar restaurant environments. Based on research into dog training best practices for public outings, I've created a structured approach including environment selection criteria, preparation protocols, training techniques for new locations, and session tracking templates. The document is designed to be used as a practical guide whether Remi is in Mexico currently or after the planned move to Tennessee.

---

## 1. New Environment Testing Strategy

### Why New Environments Matter

Testing in unfamiliar restaurant environments is a critical milestone in Remi's public behavior training because:

- **Novel stimuli exposure**: New environments present unfamiliar sounds, smells, people, and routines that test Remi's ability to remain calm
- **Generalization proof**: Calm behavior in familiar locations doesn't guarantee success in new ones—each new location requires separate proofing
- **Building confidence**: Successful experiences in varied environments build Remi's confidence and resilience
- **Real-world preparation**: As a digital nomad dog, Remi will encounter countless new environments—this testing prepares them for that reality

### Environment Selection Criteria

When choosing new restaurants to test Remi, look for these characteristics:

| Factor | Ideal for Early Sessions | Progressively More Challenging |
|--------|-------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Noise Level** | Quiet lunch hours | Busy dinner service |
| **Foot Traffic** | Minimal passing traffic | Frequent waitstaff and patron movement |
| **Space** | Spacious, corner locations | Tight spaces with more proximity |
| **Patio Type** | Enclosed/semi-private | Open to main walkway |
| **Other Dogs** | Few or no other dogs present | Multiple dogs at various tables |
| **Food Temptation** | Limited food droppage | Outdoor areas with food debris |

## 2. Recommended Restaurant Types for Testing

### Phase 1: Low-Stimulation New Environments

Start with these types of venues when introducing Remi to unfamiliar restaurants:

1. **Counter-service cafes** (Mexico: cafés de坛, taquerías informales; Tennessee: coffee shops, fast-casual)
   - Easy exit if needed
   - Typically shorter visits
   - Often more forgiving of dog behavior

2. **Breakfast/brunch spots**
   - Generally quieter than dinner service
   - More relaxed atmosphere

3. **Weekday lunches**
   - Lower crowd density
   - More predictable environment

4. **Casual dining with outdoor patios**
   - More space to maneuver
   - Can position Remi away from high-traffic areas

### Phase 2: Medium-Stimulation Environments

Once Remi demonstrates reliable calm behavior in Phase 1 locations:

1. **Busy weekend brunch spots**
2. **Breweries/taprooms** (popular in Tennessee)
3. **Family restaurants** (more activity, varied stimuli)
4. **Restaurant patios near dog-friendly parks**

### Phase 3: Higher-Challenge Environments

For advanced testing:

1. **Full-service restaurants with indoor seating**
2. **Live music venues** (outdoor patios)
3. **Food-focused events/festivals**
4. **High-end dining with patio access**

## 3. Pre-Visit Preparation Checklist

### Day-of Training Preparation

| Task | Timing | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| ☐ Exercise session | 1-2 hours before | Physical activity reduces restlessness |
| ☐ Mental stimulation | 30-60 min before | Puzzle feeder, training rep, or sniff work |
| ☐ Bathroom break | Right before arrival | Empty bladder/bladder reduces distractions |
| ☐ Grooming check | Before leaving | Clean paws, brushed coat |
| ☐ Treats prepared | At departure | High-value treats for new environments |
| ☐ Water/bowl packed | At departure | Especially important in warm climates |

### What to Bring

**Essential items:**
- Remi's regular leash + backup
- High-value treats (more appealing than usual)
- Portable water bowl
- Comfort item (favorite toy or blanket)
- Potty pads (for longer meals)
- Paper towels/cleaning supplies

**Optional but helpful:**
- Travel mat or portable bed (helps establish "place")
- Calming aid (if recommended by vet)
- Clicker (if using clicker training)
- Phone for documenting sessions

### Pre-Arrival Research

Before visiting a new restaurant:

- [ ] Call or check online to confirm dog-friendly policy
- [ ] Ask about patio layout and where dogs are permitted
- [ ] Inquire about typical busy times
- [ ] Check if there are other dogs commonly present

## 4. New Environment Training Protocol

### Arrival Protocol

1. **Park away from heavy foot traffic** if possible—enter through a less crowded entrance
2. **Allow Remi to sniff** the entrance area to decompress
3. **Walk past the patio/seating area** first without stopping to assess the environment
4. **Choose a position** away from high-traffic areas (corners, against walls)
5. **Have Remi sit** before entering the seating area

### Initial Settling (First 5-10 Minutes)

| Minute | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 0-2 | Allow Remi to settle under chair/table. Give treat for calm behavior |
| 2-4 | Remi remains calm? Treat. Watch for stress signals |
| 4-6 | Minor distractions (people walking by). Reward calm |
| 6-10 | If calm, begin extending treat intervals slightly |

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

**If Remi is overly alert/anxious:**
- Move to quieter area if possible
- Increase treat frequency initially
- Consider shorter session (10-15 min max)
- Return to familiar location and try again later

**If Remi is fixated on food/other dogs:**
- Use higher value treats
- Practice "look at me" command
- Create more distance from distraction
- Work on "leave it" foundation

**If Remi barks:**
- Immediately remove from environment
- Calm before re-attempting (or end session)
- Don't reward barking with attention

### Success Criteria for New Environment

Remi is ready to progress when in a new environment they can:

- [ ] Settle under chair/table within 2-3 minutes
- [ ] Remain calm during normal foot traffic
- [ ] Not react to other dogs at reasonable distance
- [ ] Accept treats at graduated intervals
- [ ] Remain settled for at least 20 minutes

## 6. Location-Specific Guidance

### Current: Mexico

**Dog-friendly culture in Mexico:**
- Many taquerías and casual eateries welcome dogs on patios
- Parks and outdoor areas are generally dog-friendly
- Some upscale restaurants in tourist areas allow dogs

**Tips for Mexico:**
- Early morning (before 10 AM) or late afternoon (after 3 PM) typically offers cooler temperatures and fewer crowds
- Bring plenty of water—warm weather increases hydration needs
- Many outdoor venues have concrete/metal furniture that can be hot to the touch; bring a mat
- Street noise (vendors, traffic, music) adds extra stimuli—start with quieter locations

### Destination: Tennessee

**Dog-friendly culture in Tennessee:**
- Nashville and other cities have growing dog-friendly patio scenes
- Breweries and taprooms are particularly dog-friendly
- Many restaurants in tourist areas welcome dogs on patios
- Some cities have specific dog-friendly districts

**Tips for Tennessee:**
- Knoxville, Nashville, and Chattanooga have strong dog-friendly cultures
- Summer months can be hot and humid—plan for indoor venues with good AC or early/late outdoor times
- College towns (Knoxville - UT; Nashville - Vanderbilt) often have dog-friendly areas
- Fall/winter is ideal for outdoor dining testing

## 7. Progressive Testing Schedule

### Suggested 4-Week Plan

**Week 1: Introduction to New Environments**
- 2-3 new locations (low stimulation)
- Focus on short sessions (15-20 min)
- High treat frequency
- Goal: Successful settling in 2+ new environments

**Week 2: Building Variety**
- 2-3 new locations (varying stimulation levels)
- Session duration: 20-30 minutes
- Begin extending treat intervals slightly
- Goal: Settle in 2+ different types of venues

**Week 3: Challenging Environments**
- 2-3 new locations (moderate to high stimulation)
- Session duration: 30-45 minutes
- Reduced treat frequency (2-3x initial rate)
- Goal: Maintain calm in busier environments

**Week 4: Mixed Practice**
- Mix of familiar and new locations
- Focus on reducing overall treat frequency
- Goal: Reliable calm across 3+ environment types

---

## 8. Key Takeaways

1. **Start slow**: New environments are more challenging than familiar ones—expect to provide more support initially

2. **Choose strategically**: Low-traffic, quiet times, and good positioning all increase chances of success

3. **Document everything**: Track which environments work best to inform future choices

4. **Exit gracefully**: If Remi is overwhelmed, leave calmly—setting back slightly is better than creating a negative association

5. **Celebrate small wins**: Each successful new environment is a significant milestone in Remi's training
