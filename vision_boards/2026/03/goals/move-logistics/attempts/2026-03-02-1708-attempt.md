### 3. Remi's Travel Requirements

#### For In-Cabin Travel:
- **Carrier Requirements:** 
  - Soft-sided carrier preferred
  - Maximum dimensions: 18" L x 11" W x 11" H (most airlines)
  - Must fit under seat in front of you
  - Pet must be able to stand up, turn around, and lie down

#### Health Documentation Required:
| Document | Validity | Where to Get |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **USDA-Appointed Veterinarian Health Certificate** | Within 10 days of travel | Licensed Mexican vet + USDA endorsement |
| **Rabies Vaccination Record** | Current, at least 30 days old | Vet records |
| **Microchip Verification** | Required for international | Vet |
| **Airline Pet Travel Form** | At check-in | Completed online/terminal |

#### Timeline for Health Certificate:
- Schedule vet appointment **7-10 days before departure**
- Request USDA endorsement if required (can take 2-3 business days)
- Keep copies of all documents + carry originals

### 6. Tips for Smooth Travel with Remi

1. **Book early** — Most airlines limit pets per flight (typically 1-2 in first class, 2-4 in economy)
2. **Choose direct/shortest connection** — Less stress for pet
3. **Select daytime flights** — Easier for pet adjustment
4. **Book window seat** — More floor space under seat
5. **Soft carrier** — More flexible than hard-shell
6. **Remi's comfort** — Familiar blanket, item with home scent
7. **Water** — Offer water during layover stops

### 7. Alternative: Pet Relocation Services

If the logistics feel overwhelming, consider:
- **Pet Express** (petexpress.com) — Specializes in Mexico-US
- **Airpet Relocation** — Full-service pet transport
- **Cost**: $500-1,500+ depending on service level
